Discussion: Acute Kidney Injury in Pregnancy

  • Any creatinine > 90 µmol/l in pregnancy should be considered diagnostic of AKI.
  • Management: careful fluid management, drug review, and in severe cases, renal replacement therapy (usually if serum urea >17mmol/L despite medical mx)
  • If concomittant MgSO4 used: if urine output falls to < 20 ml/hour or creatinine is > 90 µmol/l, would advise 50% dose reduction in magnesium sulphate infusion (1 g/hour to 0.5 g/hour).
  • Reference: Acute Kidney Injury in Pregnancy - TOG 2016 (http://tinyurl.com/AKIinPregnancyTOG)
