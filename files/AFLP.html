Acute Fatty Liver of Pregnancy (AFLP)

Acute fatty liver of pregnancy (AFLP) is a rare complication occurring in the third trimester, characterized by microvesicular fatty infiltration of the liver, leading to symptoms such as coagulopathy, encephalopathy, and hypoglycemia.

Management Summary

Management involves immediate supportive care and often prompt delivery to mitigate risks. Post-delivery, liver function typically normalizes within 7 to 10 days. Both the mother and the newborn should be tested for LCHAD deficiency as a predisposing factor.
