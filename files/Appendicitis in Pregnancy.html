Appendicitis in pregnancy key points
2
Appendicitis is common in pregnancy
3
The most common cause of an acute surgical abdomen in pregnancy
4
Most commonly presents in the second trimester
5
Classic presentation is of central abdominal pain that localises to the right iliac fossa. This is frequently associated with fever, nausea, diarrhoea and urinary symptoms.
6
7
Imaging
8
If the diagnosis is clear and/or the patient has abdominal pain and is haemodynamically compromised requiring laparoscopy/laprotomy the clinician should proceed to take the patient to theatre. If the diagnosis is unclear imaging may be helpful.
9
Ultrasound sensitivity for diagnosis of appendicitis in pregnancy is 67-100% with specificity of 83-96%.
10
CT sensitivity for diagnosis of appendicitis in pregnancy is 86% with specificity of 97%
11
MRI in pregnant women with suspected appendicitis confers a sensitivity of 91% and a specificity of 98%. The American College of Radiology dictates that MRI should be used in cases where ultrasound is inconclusive for appendicitis in pregnancy
12

Management and Outcomes
14
Treatment is surgery
15
Morbidity of surgery is the same as for the non-pregnant population
16
Fetal loss in simple appendicitis is 1.5%
17
Fetal loss in appendicitis with peritonitis 6%
18
Fetal loss with perforated appendix 36%
