Acute Kidney Injury in Pregnancy

Acute Kidney Injury (AKI) in pregnancy is a serious condition that requires prompt diagnosis and management to prevent adverse outcomes for both the mother and the fetus.

Causes

Common causes of AKI in pregnancy include pre-eclampsia, eclampsia, HELLP syndrome, and acute fatty liver of pregnancy.

Diagnosis

Diagnosis involves monitoring renal function tests, urine output, and imaging studies to assess kidney function and identify the underlying cause.

Management

  • Close monitoring of renal function and fluid balance.
  • Management of underlying conditions such as pre-eclampsia.
  • Consideration of renal replacement therapy if necessary.

Prevention

Preventive measures include early recognition and treatment of pre-eclampsia, proper hydration, and avoidance of nephrotoxic medications.
