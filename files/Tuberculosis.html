Key Points
4
Outcomes are the same for those with early diagnosis and treatment of tuberculosis in pregnancy compared to pregnancies unaffected by tuberculosis
5
Late diagnosis and treatment of pulmonary TB associated with pre-eclampsia, PPH, difficult labour, preterm labour, low birthweight and pneumonia.
6
Extrapulmonary TB Lymphadenitis doesn't have significant effect on perinatal outcome but other extrapulmonary sites including spine, abdomen and central nervous system are associated with increased rates of fetal growth restriction and low Apgar's.

Treatment
15
1st line for active disease is: Isoniazid, rifampicin, ethambutol and pyrazinamide.
16
1st line for latent TB: Isoniazid and rifampicin
17
Isoniazid can cause neuropathy - vitamin B6 (pyridoxine) supplementation should be offered to avoid this
