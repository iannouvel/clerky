Pharmaceutical Treatment of Menorrhagia
4
If pharmaceutical treatment is appropriate NICE advise treatments should be considered in the following order:
5
1. levonorgestrel-releasing intrauterine system (LNG-IUS) provided at least 12 months use is anticipated
6
2. tranexamic acid or NSAIDs* or combined oral contraceptives (COCs) or cyclical oral progestogens
7
3. Consider progesterone only contraception eg injected long-acting progestogens
8
9
*When heavy menstrual bleeding (HMB) coexists with dysmenorrhoea NSAIDs should be preferred to tranexamic acid. Also note NSAIDs and tranexamic are appropriate to use if treatment needed pending investigations.
10
11
Non-Pharmaceutical Management of Menorrhagia
12
Endometrial ablation can be considered where bleeding is having a severe impact on a woman's quality of life, and she does not want to conceive in the future. Appropriate to use in patients with small fibroids (<3cm)
13
UAE, myomectomy or hysterectomy should be considered in cases of HMB where large fibroids (>3 cm) are present and bleeding is having a severe impact on a woman's quality of life.

Hysterectomy should not be used as a first-line treatment solely for HMB.
18
19
Hysterectomy should be considered only when:
20
other treatment options have failed, are contraindicated or are declined by the woman
21
there is a wish for amenorrhoea
22
the woman (who has been fully informed) requests it
23
the woman no longer wishes to retain her uterus and fertility.
