Endometrial Hyperplasia without atypiaSource GTG 67
2
3
Summary:
4
Progression to cancer <5% over 20 years (1% in simple EH, 4% in complex EH)
Majority regress spontaneously, consider weight loss if obese and advise (if appropriate) to discontinue HRT
Progestogen treatment for at least 6 months - has higher disease regression rate than conservative management, advise more important if peristent hyperplasia during surveillance or if having AUB
IUS 1st line treatment (continuous oral progesterones alternative but regression rate lower), if effective advise to continue for 5 years as lower relapse rate, particularly if also having AUB
If declines IUS - advise continuous progestogen regimes like medroxyprogesterone 10–20 mg/day or norethisterone 10–15 mg/day
Endometrial surveillance with outpatient biopsy every 6 months
Once treated (ie biopsy negative), higher risk relapse patients (BMI>35 and/or had oral progestogens) advise 6-monthly until 2 consecutive negative samples, then offer yearly biopsy
Hysterectomy may be considered especially if treatment failure
Endometrial ablation is not recommended

Criteria for hyseterctomy if EH without atypia
(i) progression to atypical hyperplasia occurs during follow-up, or
(ii) there is no histological regression of hyperplasia despite 12 months of treatment, or
(iii) there is relapse of endometrial hyperplasia after completing progestogen treatment, or
(iv) there is persistence of bleeding symptoms, or
(v) the woman declines to undergo endometrial surveillance or comply with medical treatment.
