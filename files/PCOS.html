Diagnosis
4
Rotterdam Criteria - at least 2 of the following:
5
1. Oligo/anovulation (< 10 periods per year is considered the threshold for some sources)
6
2. Hyperandrogenism (biochemical or clinical (hirsute / acne) -
Free androgen index should be calculated to assess the amount of physiologically active testosterone present.
Luteinizing hormone, follicle-stimulating hormone, prolactin, and thyroid-stimulating hormone levels should be measured to rule out other causes of oligomenorrhoea and amenorrhoea (such as premature ovarian failure, hypothyroidism, and hyperprolactinaemia).
7
3. Polycystic ovaries on ultrasound -
polycystic ovaries include >12 antral follicles and/or increased size (>10cm3)
in adolescents it is much more common to have a multifollicular appearance and the diagnosis considered with caution

Counselling
10
Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS) is a condition in which the ovaries produce an abnormal amount of androgens, male sex hormones that are usually present in women in small amounts.
The name polycystic ovary syndrome describes the numerous small cysts (fluid-filled sacs) that form in the ovaries.
However, some women with this disorder do not have cysts, while some women without the disorder do develop cysts.
Ovulation occurs when a mature egg is released from an ovary.
This happens so it can be fertilized by a male sperm.
If the egg is not fertilized, it is sent out of the body during your period.
In some cases, a woman doesn’t make enough of the hormones needed to ovulate.
When ovulation doesn’t happen, the ovaries can develop many small cysts.
These cysts make hormones called androgens.
Women with PCOS often have high levels of androgens.
This can cause more problems with a woman’s menstrual cycle.
And it can cause many of the symptoms of PCOS.
Treatment for PCOS is often done with medication.
This can’t cure PCOS, but it helps reduce symptoms and prevent some health problems.

Complications
13
Metabolic disorders, such as impaired glucose tolerance and type 2 diabetes.
Cardiovascular disease.
Infertility.
Pregnancy complications, such as pre-eclampsia and gestational diabetes.
Endometrial cancer.
Psychological disorders, such as anxiety and depression.
Obstructive sleep apnoea

Management
16
Counselling: explain aetiology (hyperandrogenism and associated hormonal dysregulation including oligo/amenorrhoea)
17
Discuss diagnostic criteria and complications of diagnosis: Metabolic disorders, such as impaired glucose tolerance and type 2 diabetes, Cardiovascular disease, Infertility, Pregnancy complications, such as pre-eclampsia and gestational diabetes.
Endometrial cancer. Psychological disorders, such as anxiety and depression. Obstructive sleep apnoea
18
Pregnancy: screen for diabetes pre-pregnancy and at 24-28 weeks
19
Diabetes: screen if BMI > 25 or if other risk factors present (age > 40, GDM, FHx)
20
OSA: ask if symptomatic, consider screening questions
21
CVD risk: ask regarding particular factors (HTN / FHx / obesity / lack of exercise / dyslipidaemia / diabetes / GDM)
22
Treatment: treat hypertension, refer dyslipidaemia to specilist for tx
23
Depression / anxiety: screen and if suggestive, assess.
Also consider associated psychologial conditions (ie psychosexual / eating disorder / negative body image)
24
Cancer risk: advise needs menses at least every 3-4 months, prescribe 'gestogens' if required to induce withdrawal bleed
25
USS: if oligo/amenorrhoea, consider TVUSS to assess ET and if <10 mm reassure as unlikely to have hyperplasia
NB If prolonged amenorrhoea, use a cyclica progestogen (eg medroxyprogestogen 10mg OD for 14 days) to induce a withdrawal bleed prior to assessing ET
26
Endometrial protection therapy - options include:
1. cyclical progestogen, such as medroxyprogesterone 10 mg daily for 14 days every 1–3 months
2. low-dose combined oral contraceptive (COC)
3. Mirena / LNG-IUS
If not keen on hormones then regular (6-12 months) ultrasonographic ET assessment
27
Acne - hormones / retinoids / antibiotics
28
Hirsutism - shaving / waxing / topical treatments, consider COC (particularly Dianette)
29
First line: diet, exercise and weight loss advice prior to pharmacology including referral to dietitican if BMI > 25
30
Second line: ovarian drilling adviseable if normal BMI and anovulatory to induce ovulation
31
Bariatric surgery: adviseable if BMI > 40 or >35 with obesity related health conditions as second line if standard weight loss regimes have failed.
