Epidemiology
4
10 deaths per year in the UK.
5
Severe sepsis with acute organ dysfunction has a mortality rate of 20–40%, rising to around 60% if septicaemic shock develops

Risk Factors
8
Obesity / Impaired glucose tolerance/diabetes Impaired immunity / immunosuppressant medication / Anaemia / Vaginal discharge / History of pelvic infection / Amniocentesis and other invasive procedures / Cervical cerclage / Prolonged spontaneous rupture of membranes / Vaginal trauma / Caesarean section / Wound haematoma / Retained products of conception / GAS infection in close contacts / family members / Black or minority ethnic group origin
9
Risk factors for GAS: recent or close contact with sore throat / impetigo / pharyngitis / URTI / LRTI / cellulitis
10
Causative Bacteria
11
GAS, also known as Streptococcus pyogenes ● Escherichia coli ● Staphylococcus aureus ● Streptococcus pneumoniae ● meticillin-resistant S. aureus (MRSA), Clostridium septicum and Morganella morganii.
12
13
Prevention
14
Group A strep (pyogenes) identified in the pregnancy should be treated aggressively
15
16
Management
17
Admit if unwell, dehydrated or vomiting
18
Consider all sources: endometritis / mastitis / UTI / pneumonia / skin & soft tissue / pharyngitis / regional anaesthetic entry / gastroenteritis
19
Indications for IV abx and senior review: abdominal pain / fever (>38) / tachycardia (>90)
20
Avoid NSAIDs during sepsis as they inhibit the immune system (particularly polymorphs attacking GAS)
21
If diagnosis is of sepsis - discuss with clinical microbiologist / ID specialist, send appropriate micro samples and start abx within 1 hour
22
If previously diagnosed as carrier of resistant strains, discuss with infection control team
23
If necrotising fascitis suspected, discuss with ITU and surgical teams (ideally plastics/reconstructive specilalist)
24
Measure lactate and if >4 this indicates tissue hypoperfusion, give 20ml/kg crystalloid and if unresponsive give vasopressors, monitor vasopressors with central venous pressures (>8) or oxygenation (>70 for venous, >65 for mixed)
25
Consider CXR / pelvic USS / CT
26
Consider throat / nasal swabs
27
Abx - if severe, consider Tazocin or meropenem and clindamycin
28
If MRSA resistant (or highly likely to be), consider adding vancomycin or teicoplanin
29
IVIG if diagnosis is likely to be strep or staph and other treatments have failed
30
Treat mum and baby if in the early neonatal period, GAS suspected and inspect baby's umbilicus
