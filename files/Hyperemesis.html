Criteria for admission (any of the below)
15
Continued nausea and vomiting and inability to keep down oral antiemetics
16
Continued nausea and vomiting associated with ketonuria and/or weight loss (greater than 5% of body weight), despite oral antiemetics
17
Confirmed or suspected comorbidity (such as urinary tract infection and inability to tolerate oral antibiotics).
18
19
Antiemetic treatment considerations
20
1st line: Antihistamines (ie cyclizine) and phenothiazine (ie prochlorperazine)
21
Dual agent: consider dual or multiple agents if required
22
Route: consider parenteral or rectal
23
2nd line: Ondansetron (due to limited safety data / concern regarding orofacial cleft and cardiac development - safe after 10 weeks)
24
Refractory: steroids should be considered for N&V, consider MDT involvment, consider therapeutic TOP if all else fails
25
Not indicated: pyridoxine, diazepam
26
27
Fluid therapy:
28
1st line: N saline with K, daily U&Es
29
2nd line: dextrose - only if sodium levels normal and thiamine has been given (risk of Wernicke-Korsakoff syndrome)
