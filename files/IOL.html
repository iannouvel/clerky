Induction rationales
Post-dates: after 41 weeks incr risk EMCS / neonatal stay / stillbirth / neonatal death, incr stillbirth rate, higher in ethnic minorities, particular black women, if declines IOL for postdates, twice weekly CTG and LV
PPROM: only <34 weeks if other indications (suspected chorio / fetal distress), if GBS -ve, aim for 37 weeks, if GBS+ve, if PPROM after 34 weeks immediate IOL/CS, if PPROM <34 weeks discuss timing with senior
PROM: if all investigations (CTG / liquor / symptoms / fetal movements) normal, offer expectant or immediate IOL, advise IOL after 24 hours, if GBS+ve in this pregnancy, advise immediate IOL / CS
Previous C-secton: IOL incr risk EMCS / rupture, particularly with hormonal IOL and augmentation, approx 1:100 risk rupture, offer IOL / ERCS, advise can have expectant mx
Maternal request: highly individualised and circumstance-specific, recurrent DFM without evidence of maternal / fetal comporomise is often timed akin to mat request IOL ie 39 weeks
Diabetes in pregnancy: as per NICE GDM guildeline - Offer delivery at 37 to 39 weeks for T1DM / T2DM, advise women with uncomplicated gestational diabetes to give birth no later than 40 weeks plus 6 days
Marcosomia without diabetes: advise significantly lower risk SD, slightly higher risk OASI, no difference in perinatal death / BPI / EMCS
Intrauterine fetal death: Offer IOL / CS / Expectant, if membranes rupture / unwell / bleeding / PET - advise immediate IOL/CS
if IOL - 1-to-1 care and manual monitoring of contractions
If IOL and unscarred uterus, offer mife or mechanical
If IOL and scarred uterus, explain risk of rupture, mife and dino are contraindicated
Relative contraindicaiton to IOL: previous precipitant birth - not appropriate to offer IOL to avoid unattended birth
Absolute contraindications to IOL: fetal growht restriction and compromise - advise immediate / urgent CS depending on circumnstances
Sweeps - Explain process, pain / bleeding as risks, routinely from 39 weeks, earlier if reasonable
Method of IOL
If BS < 6, offer vaginal dinoprostone or low dose oral miso (25 ug) and consider mechanical (balloon or osmotic dilator) if pharmacological contraindicated / not suitable
If BS > 6, offer amniotomy and immediate synto, if declines synto in order to establish, advise longer labour and higher risk neonatal infection
Unsuccessful IOL: Consider options: rest / expectant / further IOL / CS
