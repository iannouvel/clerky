Summary stepwise management
Lifestyle measures: fluid / diet (avoidance of irritants / artificial things) / constipation / smoking / stress / exercise - signpost to bladder health UK
Bladder retraining: timed voids with pausing for 5-15 minutes when urge present - signpost to leaflets
PFE: help strengthen the muscles which hold onto urine - signpost to website
Meds: antimuscarinics / mirabegron / consider vaginal oestrogen and desmopressin, discuss chance of success, side effects, avoid oxybutynin if frail, transdermal if oral not tolerated, review in 4 weeks
If above fails, consider urodynamics
Botox / Neuromodulation / Augmentation cystoplasty

Before starting treatment with a medicine for overactive bladder, explain to the patient:
12
Approx 50-65% of patients find medications at least partially beneficial
1. the likelihood of the medicine being successful
13
2. the common adverse effects associated with the medicine
14
3. that some adverse effects of anticholinergic medicines, such as dry mouth and constipation, may indicate that the medicine is starting to have an effect
15
4. that she may not see substantial benefits until she has been taking the medicine for at least 2 weeks and that her symptoms may continue to improve over time
16
5. that the long-term effects of anticholinergic medicines for overactive bladder on cognitive function are uncertain

Choosing medicine
26
Do not offer women flavoxate, propantheline or imipramine to treat urinary incontinence or overactive bladder. [2013]
27
Do not offer oxybutynin (immediate release) to older women who may be at higher risk of a sudden deterioration in their physical or mental health.
28
Offer the anticholinergic medicine with the lowest acquisition cost to treat overactive bladder or mixed urinary incontinence in women. [2019]
29
If the first medicine for overactive bladder or mixed urinary incontinence is not effective or well-tolerated, offer another medicine with a low acquisition cost
30
Offer a transdermal overactive bladder treatment to women unable to tolerate oral medicines. [2013]

Reviewing medicine
40
Offer a face-to-face or telephone review 4 weeks after starting a new medicine for overactive bladder. Ask the woman if she is satisfied with the treatment}} and if improvement is optimal, continue treatment and if there is no or suboptimal improvement, or intolerable adverse effects, change the dose or try an alternative medicine for overactive bladder (see the recommendations for when the first medicine is not effective and offering a transdermal patch in the section on choosing medicines), and review again 4 weeks later.
