Advice:
Two types of HSV: Type 1 and 2. Type 2 accounts for 70% of genital herpes infections. HSV infection may be transmitted to neonates. Transmission is typically due to the neonate coming into contact with infected maternal secretions during delivery (transplacental infection reported but very rare). Highest risk with primary herpes infection within 6 weeks of delivery. Viral shedding can continue after lesions have healed.. Neonatal herpes is rare (UK incidence 3/100,000 live births) but serious.

HSV in Pregnancy - 1st or 2nd trimster acquisition
Advice: Two types of HSV: Type 1 and 2. Type 2 accounts for 70% of genital herpes infections. HSV infection may be transmitted to neonates. Refer to GUM for confirmation / screening / management plan
Initial episode treated acicolvir 400 mg TDS for 5 days - decreases duration / severity and viral shedding
Symptomatic therapy - paracetamol and topical lidocaine 2%
Following 1st or 2nd trimester acquisition, daily suppressive aciclovir 400 mg TDS from 36 weeks of gestation reduces HSV lesions at term and hence the need for delivery by caesarean section
If acquisition is 6 weeks from delivery, advise C-section to reduce neonatal HSV infection

HSV in Pregnancy - Primary genital herpes in preterm prelabour rupture of membranes (PPROM)
HSV in Pregnancy - Primary genital herpes in preterm prelabour rupture of membranes (PPROM)
Advice: Two types of HSV: Type 1 and 2. Type 2 accounts for 70% of genital herpes infections. HSV infection may be transmitted to neonates. Transmission is typically due to the neonate coming into contact with infected maternal secretions during delivery (transplacental infection reported but very rare). Highest risk with primary herpes infection within 6 weeks of delivery. Viral shedding can continue after lesions have healed.. Neonatal herpes is rare (UK incidence 3/100,000 live births) but serious.
Limited evidence in this particular situation
Confer with MDT (obs / neonates / GUM) with gestation being the over-riding factor
If delivery indicated, advise C-section.
If conservative mx mother should be recommended to receive intravenous aciclovir 5 mg/kg every 8 hours
Consider prophylactic corticosteroids reduce the implications of preterm delivery upon the infant.
If delivery is indicated within 6 weeks of the primary infection, delivery by caesarean section may still offer some benefit despite the prolonged rupture of membranes.

HSV in Pregnancy - Recurrent genital herpes in PPROM
HSV in Pregnancy - Recurrent genital herpes in PPROM
Advice: Two types of HSV: Type 1 and 2. Type 2 accounts for 70% of genital herpes infections. HSV infection may be transmitted to neonates. Transmission is typically due to the neonate coming into contact with infected maternal secretions during delivery (transplacental infection reported but very rare). Highest risk with primary herpes infection within 6 weeks of delivery. Viral shedding can continue after lesions have healed.. Neonatal herpes is rare (UK incidence 3/100,000 live births) but serious.
Risk of neonatal transmission is very small and may be outweighed by the morbidity and mortality associated with premature delivery.
In the case of PPROM before 34 weeks there is evidence to suggest that expectant management is appropriate, including oral aciclovir 400 mg three times daily for the mother.
After 34 weeks gestation, it is recommended that management is undertaken in accordance with relevant RCOG guidelines on PPROM and antenatal corticosteroid administration to reduce neonatal morbidity and mortality and is not materially influenced by the presence of recurrent genital herpes lesions.
