4
Definition
Obesity is defined as a body mass index >30kg/m², subclassified into types I (BMI 30-35), II (35-40) and III (40+)

Risks
Women with obesity are at increased risk of: miscarriage, GDM, PET, VET, IOL, CS, anaesthetics and wound complications, reduced BF
Babies of obese mothers are at increased risk of: stillbirth, congenital anomalies, PTB, macrosomia, NND, metabolic disorders in childhood

Interventions for pregnant obese women
Folic acid 5mg daily
Vitamin D 10micrograms daily
LMWH: If BMI > 40, they all need it postnatally and mabye antenatallly
A woman with a BMI >40 should have the anaesthetic team involved in her care. It is advised the duty anaesthetist is informed when the patient is admitted to labour ward if delivery is expected. These patients should have early venous access and be assessed for suitability of regional blocked.
Women with a BMI >35 should have surveillance for pre-eclampsia
Should have a 2 hour 75g oral glucose tolerance test at 24-28 weeks
Women with a BMI > 40 should give birth in a consultant led obstetric unit and have continuous midwifery throughout labour and avoidance of pressure sores
Should be recommended to consider CEFM and be advised to have active management of the third stage of labour
