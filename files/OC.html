4
Considered ICP (aka OC) if:
a) pregnant
b) itch with normal skin
c) peak random BA of 19 or more
If either severe OR early OR atypical - discuss with hepatologist
Confirm diagnosis postnatally with normalisation of symptoms and bloods
In isolated ICP and singleton pregnancy - risk of SB only increased if BA > 100.
If peak BA 19-39, diagnose mild ICP, consider delivery by 40/40
If peak BA 40–99, diagnose moderate ICP, if no other RF advise them that the known risk of stillbirth is similar to the background risk until 38–39 weeks' gestation and consider planned birth at 38–39 weeks' gestation
If peak BA > 100, diagnose severe ICP and advise them that the risk of stillbirth is higher than the background risk, consider planned birth at 35–36 weeks' gestation
If ICP and twins: risk of stillbirth is higher compared with a twin pregnancy without ICP
USS / CTG doesn't improve outcomes
If GDM / PET / twins AND ICP - advise higher risk SB and adjust timing of birth accordingly
Tx: advise no tx improves outcomes or reduces peak BA levels, tx for itch of limited benefit, urso of uncertain safety, advise urso not routinely given since 2022
