Congenital absence of the upper two-thirds of the vagina and the uterus is absent or rudimentary.
Common presentation is primary amenorrhoea in a female with 46,XX karyotype and normal secondary sexual characteristics.
There may be associated abnormalities of the kidneys, skeletal system, heart and auditory system.
Magnetic resonance imaging is a useful diagnostic tool with which to assess the anatomical abnormalities.
Management involves psychological support and creation of a neovagina for sexual function
