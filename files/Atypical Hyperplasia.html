Atypical Hyperplasia

Source: GTG 67

Summary:

  • Risk of progression ~ 40%
  • If declines surgery:
    • Explain risks
    • Consider imaging to rule out invasive Ca, discuss with MDT
    • Advise IUS, 2nd line continuous oral progestogens
    • 3-monthly endometrial surveillance until 2 consecutive negative samples
    • Histological disease regression = 2 consecutive negative samples
    • If has histological disease regression, long-term follow-up is 6-12 month biopsy until hysterectomy
    • If desires fertility, advise at least 1 negative sample prior to conception AND refer to fertility

Major Risk Factors

  • Oestrogen (HRT)
  • Tamoxifen
  • PCOS
  • Obesity
  • Immunosuppression (transplant)

Atypical Hyperplasia Key Points

  • Total hysterectomy indicated as risk of underlying malignancy or progression to cancer (studies show progression rates are 30-50%)
  • IUS or oral progesterones alternatives for women who decline surgery
