Hypertension in Pregnancy

Hypertension during pregnancy can present in several forms and poses significant risks to both mother and baby. Early diagnosis and management are crucial for the best outcomes.

Diagnosis

  • Chronic Hypertension: Hypertension present at the booking visit, before 20 weeks, or if the woman is already on antihypertensive medication.
  • Gestational Hypertension: New hypertension presenting after 20 weeks without significant proteinuria.
  • Preeclampsia (PET): New onset hypertension after 20 weeks with proteinuria or other maternal organ dysfunction such as renal insufficiency, liver involvement, neurological complications, haematological complications, or uteroplacental dysfunction.
  • Severe Hypertension: Blood pressure over 160/110 mmHg.
  • Severe PET: PET with severe hypertension not responding to treatment, or associated with significant symptoms or lab abnormalities.

Management

Pre-pregnancy Advice

Women with chronic hypertension should receive counseling on the risks of hypertension in pregnancy and the importance of blood pressure control before conception.

Antenatal Treatment

  • Monitor blood pressure regularly.
  • Administer antihypertensive medication if necessary.
  • Regular fetal assessment to monitor growth and wellbeing.

Timing of Birth

  • Before 37 weeks: Continue surveillance unless there are significant risks to the mother or baby.
  • 37 weeks onwards: Initiate birth within 24 to 48 hours.

Postnatal Treatment

  • Measure blood pressure frequently initially.
  • Adjust antihypertensive treatment as needed.
  • Medical review at 2 and 6-8 weeks post-birth.
