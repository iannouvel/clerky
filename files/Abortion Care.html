Abortion Care

Abortion care involves providing safe, legal, and compassionate options for terminating a pregnancy. Healthcare providers should ensure informed consent, support, and follow-up care.

Types of Abortion

There are two main types of abortion: medical and surgical. The choice depends on the gestational age and the woman's preference.

Medical Abortion

Medical abortion involves taking medications to end a pregnancy. It is typically used up to 10 weeks of gestation.

  • Mifepristone followed by Misoprostol is the most common regimen.
  • Follow-up is necessary to ensure the abortion is complete.

Surgical Abortion

Surgical abortion involves a procedure to remove the pregnancy from the uterus. It can be performed at various stages of pregnancy.

  • Manual Vacuum Aspiration (MVA) is used up to 12 weeks.
  • Dilation and Evacuation (D&E) is used after 12 weeks.

Post-Abortion Care

Post-abortion care includes managing complications, providing contraception, and offering emotional support.
