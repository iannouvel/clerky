Preconception Advice (ie only for T1 and T2)
Conservative measures improve outcomes (diet / weight / exercise), if BMI > = 27 advise weight loss pre-pregnancy
Meds: folic acid 5mg while trying until 12/40, stop ACEi/ARBs when trying, stop statins when pregnant, MF and insulins safe in pregnancy, signpost to BUMPS, advise contraception until HbA1c optimised and high-dose folic acid optimised
Screening:
Retinopathy - proliferative retinopathy predictive of foetal outcome and risk of progression (10%, if absent and 50%, if present), if found, retinopathy requiring treatment should be dealt with prior to pregnancy
Nephropathy screening - U&Es (high creatinine is associated with poorer pregnancy outcomes), urine for proteinuria estimation and creatinine clearance
Consider the possibility of macrovascular disease (e.g. IHD, CVA) and formally investigate if it is a possibility.
Monthly HbA1c while trying, USS at 7-9 weeks to determine viability and book with ANC / diabetic midwives

GDM Advice
Discuss rationale for screening - avoidance of macrosomia and shoulder dystocia
Risk factors for screening: raised BMI, previous macrosomic baby, previous GDM, FHx, ethnicity with higher prevalence)
Glycosuria - screen for GDM if 2+ glucose 1 time or 1+ glucose 2 times
Testing - OGTT if the patient has risk factors, if previous GDM then either OGTT or easy self-monitoring of blood glucose
Diagnosis - fasting >= 5.6 or 2-hour glucose >= 7.8

Mx of GDM:
Explain that good control (through diet / exercise / and maybe medicines) improves the following outcomes: reduce the risk of fetal macrosomia, trauma during birth (for her and her baby), induction of labour and/or caesarean section, neonatal hypoglycaemia, perinatal death
MDT for GDM: dietician, diabetologist, specialist midwife
Indications for insulin (with / without metformin): fasting > 7 OR fasting b/t 6 and 7 AND macrosomia or polyhydramnios

Glucose targets for all diabetes in pregnancy: fasting < 5.3 mmol/litre, 1 hour after meals: < 7.8 mmol/litre OR 2 hours after meals: < 6.4 mmol/litre, always > 4

If diagnosed GDM, measure HbA1c at diagnosis to assess for pre-existing
