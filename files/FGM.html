FGM
2
Key points
FGM can have a lasting physical, mental and psychosexual effect on the girl or woman. •
FGM is recognised as a human rights violation and is illegal in the UK. •
There is support available for you if you or anyone you know may be affected by FGM. •
Depending on the degree of cutting (type of FGM), you may be offered a minor operation called deinfibulation to allow intercourse or vaginal birth.
Legal
Female Genital Mutilation Act 2003: FGM is illegal unless necessary for her wellbeing (physical / mental / pregnancy) including arranging it and failing to protect from it. If suspected to have occured to under 18, MUST be reported to police within 1 month.
Symptoms
Physical problems may include: • painful periods • pain while passing urine • urinary tract infections • inability or difficulty in having a vaginal examination and cervical smear • pain during sex or unable to have sex • genital scarring • vaginal narrowing • reduced sexual pleasure • problems with childbirth • increased risk of caesarean section • increased risk of bleeding after delivery • higher risk of stillbirth.
Long-term psychological problems may include: • low self-esteem • depression • anxiety • flashbacks • post-traumatic stress disorder • other mental health issues
Mx
If not pregnant and type 3 - discuss deinfibulation
If pregnant?
Antenatal care: ANC with specialist that cares for wormen w FGM, examination and discussion regarding impact. Possibly advise surgery either mid 2nd trimester or during labour / C-section. Illegal to reinfibulate.
Labour: Supported with normal labour, may have issues with progress / PPH, if for theatre and type 3 may have deinfibulation then, may have difficulty with things like VE / catheters / FSE / delayed 2nd stage / laceration / anterior episiotomy
Postnatal: Can have postnatal GOPD for discussion re deinfibulation, if female daughters we will discuss protecting them from FGM.
