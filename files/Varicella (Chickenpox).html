Varicella Zoster is the virus responsible for Chicken pox and shingles.
4
As over 90% of the UK adult population is immune to chickenpox VSV in pregnancy is rare.
5
3 in 1000 pregnancies are complicated by primary VZV infection
6
Incidence of varicella pneumonia - The green top guidelines quote historical figures at 10-14% but clarify that a more recent study has shown pneumonia complicates 5% of cases.
7
If maternal infection occurs 1 to 4 weeks before delivery up to 50% of babies are infected yet only 23% develop clinical varicella.

If Chickenpox exposure occurs during pregnancy the Green Top Guidelines advise the following:
11
Non-immune patients should be offered VZIG ASAP if up to 10 days from initial contact.
12
VZIG has no therapeutic benefit once chickenpox has developed and should therefore not be used in pregnant women who have developed a chickenpox rash.
13
Should be considered infectious from 8 days post exposure until either 21 days post exposure (if no VZIG given) or 28 days post exposure (if VZIG given).
14
Intravenous aciclovir should be given to all pregnant women with severe chickenpox
15
Oral aciclovir should be prescribed for pregnant women with chickenpox if they present within 24 hours of the onset of the rash and if they are 20+0 weeks of gestation or beyond. Use of aciclovir before 20+0 weeks should also be considered
