13
If severe PMS, consider CBT, consider SSRIs and consider GnRH analogues (the latter only for the most severe PMS and/or short courses to diagnose PMS). Also, eventually consider TLH/BSO if medical mx failed or will require long-term GnRH analogues or other gynae reason for TLH-BSO

18

If on estradiol for PMS, advise uncertainty regarding effect of treatment on breast and endometrial tissue regarding long-term risk of ***
20
If treating with danazol for luteal phase breast symptoms, advise risk of virilising effects and risk of pregnancy
21
If using GnRH analogues for >= 6 months, advise add-back therapy appropriate
22
Add-back therapy should be either continuous combined HRT or tibolone
23
Routinely advise women on the effect of exercise, diet and smoking on bone mineral density
24
Progestogens (including LNG-IUS) are not appropriate in isolation for PMS symptoms
25
SSRIs can be used either only in the luteal phase or continuously, ideally the former with newer agents
26
If SSRIs given continuously, when discontinuing advise gradual withdrawal
27
If SSRIs given, advise S/E include nausea, insomnia, somnolence, fatigue and decr libido
28
If SSRIs given, advise PMS sx should improve in pregnancy and therefore to discontinue in pregnancy
29
If SSRIs given, advise no evidence of associated congenital malformations, and if there is an effect, likely to be extremely small
30
Spironolactone can be given for 'physical' symptoms
31
In severe PMS, consider use of GnRH as a 'test-of-cure' to see if TLH-BSO would be warranted
32
If having TLH-BSO for PMS, advise HRT, particularly if <45 yrs old
33
Endometrial ablation and/or TLH with ovarian conservation are not appropriate for PMS Tx
34
