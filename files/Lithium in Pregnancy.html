Lithium in Pregnancy

Risk of fetal heart malformations when lithium is taken in the first trimester
5
Higher risk of Ebsteins anomaly (right ventricular outflow obstruction) but now thought to be much lower than previously thought (0.6% of lithium exposed infants in large 2017 study - see link to NEJM)
6
Consider stopping over 4 weeks
7
If continued check plasma lithium levels every 4 weeks, then weekly from the 36th week
8
Plan for birth in hospital
9
Stop lithium during labour and check plasma lithium levels 12 hours after her last dose
10
Several lithium side effects on neonates have been reported (include floppy muscles, tachycardia, lethargy, jaundice). The rates of these are not known. Maternal lithium clearance is known to decrease near labour. it is for these reasons monitoring is increased and lithium is stopped prior to labour.
