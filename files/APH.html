Antepartum Hemorrhage (APH)

Antepartum hemorrhage (APH) refers to bleeding from the genital tract after 24 weeks of pregnancy and before the birth of the baby.

Definitions

  • Spotting: Staining, streaking, or blood spotting noted on underwear or sanitary protection.
  • Minor Hemorrhage: Blood loss less than 50 ml that has settled.
  • Major Hemorrhage: Blood loss of 50â€“1000 ml, with no signs of clinical shock.
  • Massive Hemorrhage: Blood loss greater than 1000 ml and/or signs of clinical shock.

Prevention

APH cannot be predicted. Women should be advised, encouraged, and helped to change modifiable risk factors (such as smoking and drug misuse).

Management

It is recommended that women be advised to report all vaginal bleeding to their antenatal care provider.

  • Women with APH presenting to a midwifery-led maternity unit, a general practitioner, or an accident and emergency department should be assessed, stabilized if necessary, and transferred to a hospital maternity unit with facilities for resuscitation and performing emergency operative delivery.
  • A multidisciplinary team including midwifery and obstetric staff, with immediate access to laboratory, theatre, neonatal, and anaesthetic services, should provide clinical assessment.

Admission

Women presenting with spotting who are no longer bleeding and where placenta previa has been excluded can go home after a reassuring initial clinical assessment. All other cases should be admitted for further evaluation and management.
