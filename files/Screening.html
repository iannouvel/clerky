HIV:
Affects over 100,000 people in the United Kingdom (PHE 2015).
Transmitted through contact with blood / infected fluids, so sex / IVDU / vertical transmission are the common routes of exposure.
Vertical most likely during vaginal delivery, can also occur antenatally and through breastfeeding.
Mother to child transmission rates range from 15% to 45%
combination antiretroviral therapy (cART) development means that HIV can be managed effectively as a chronic condition and many HIV positive adultsâ€™ life expectancy will be similar to an uninfected individual.
Some higher-risk patients may benefit from repeat screening in later pregnancy.

Hep B:
In England, around 0.4% of women have hepatitis B
Mother to child transmission can be prevented in 90 to 96% of cases through the use of antiviral therapy, vaccination and hepatitis B specific immunoglobulin
