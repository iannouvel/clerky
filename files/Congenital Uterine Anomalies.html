Congenital anomalies of the uterus (such as didelphus, bicornuate, septate) are associated with miscarriage, preterm birth, growth restriction and malpresentation.
4
In light of that, it would be important to be aware of symptoms of preterm birth and present quickly should they arise, arrange serial ultrasound scan in the 3rd trimester and be aware that if malpresentation were to arise, most clinicians consider it a contraindication to ECV ('spinning the baby' to head-down) and advise a C-section.
