Diagnosis
2
In women >45 years old diagnosis peri-menopause on clinical symptoms i.e. vasomotor instability and irregular periods
3
In women >45 years old diagnose menopause on absence of periods for 12 months if not taking contraception
4
In women <45 consider using FSH if symptoms peri-menopause/menopause (be aware inaccurate if taking hormonal contraception)

Symptoms
7
vasomotor symptoms (for example, hot flushes and sweats)
8
musculoskeletal symptoms (for example, joint and muscle pain)
9
effects on mood (for example, low mood)
10
urogenital symptoms (for example, vaginal dryness)
11
sexual difficulties (for example, low sexual desire).

Treatment
14
Vasomotor symptoms
HRT (with progesterone if has uterus)
15
Psychological symtpoms
Offer HRT and/or CBT, advise SSRIs and SNRIs are not effective in the absence of formal depression
16
Low libido
Consider testosterone if HRT not sufficient to treat low libido
17
Urogenital atrophy
Topical oestrogen, if contraindicated lubricants
