Passive 2nd stage: the finding of full dilatation of the cervix before or in the absence of involuntary expulsive contractions
Onset of the active second stage of labour is either:
a) the baby is visible
b) expulsive contractions with a finding of full dilatation of the cervix OR other signs of full dilatation of the cervix
c) active maternal effort following confirmation of full dilatation of the cervix in the absence of expulsive contractions.
Obs in 2nd stage: mat HR every 15 mins, half hourly assessment of freq of contractions, hourly BP, 4 hourly temp, hourly VE in active 2nd (or if woman wishes for it)
IA every 5 mins (with mat HR every 15 to distinguish)
Nulliparous: expect birth within 3 hours of active 2nd stage, suspect delay if inadequate descent/rotation after 1 hour of active 2nd, diagnose delay if 2 hours of active 2nd and birth not imminient.

Multiparous: expect birth within 2 hours of active 2nd stage, suspect delay if inadequate descent/rotation after 30 mins of active 2nd, diagnose delay in 1 hour of active and birth not imminent
If delay suspected and membrans intact, offer VE and amniotomy
If diagnosed, refer to obstetrician
If passive 2nd diagnosed without urge to push, offer futher assessment/VE in 1 hour
If nulliparous women consider 2nd stage syntocinon if contractions inadequate at onset of 2nd stage
Avoid supine / semi-supine positions
Encourage woman to be guided by her urge to push
Offer support such as position change / bladder emptyig / encouragement
Employ perineal protection in line with protocols
