Amenorrhoea

Primary Amenorrhoea aka AIS aka androgen insensitivity syndrome

Definitions:

Primary amenorrhoea refers to a failure of menstruation by the age of 15 years (some sources say 16) in the presence of normal secondary sexual characteristics, or 13 years (some say 14) in the absence of other evidence of puberty.

Secondary amenorrhoea is defined as absent periods for at least 6 months in a woman who has previously had regular periods, or 12 months if she has previously had oligomenorrhoea (bleeds less frequently than 6-weekly).

History:

Personal developmental, medical, sexual, and familial history

Examination:

General examination, skin (CAH/PCOS), neck (T4), chest (45XO), abdomen (undescended testes in inguinal hernia), vulva, vagina, bimanual examination

Investigations:

Test bHCG (urine or serum), FSH, LH, mid-luteal progesterone (if secondary oligo), prolactin, thyroid, testosterone, estrogen/progestogen challenge test, FAI, USS, MRI, HSG/HyCoSy/lap & dye, karyotype. In order of importance: bHCG, FSH, prolactin, USS
