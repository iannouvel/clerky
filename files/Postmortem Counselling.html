A post-mortem examination changes the primary cause of death in 9-34% of stillbirths and confirms the diagnosis in 48.9%- 58%
4
Evidence alludes to the fact that completing a post-mortem examination will provide significant clinical findings in 22-76% of cases and is the single most useful investigation in providing information to the parents about why their child may have died
