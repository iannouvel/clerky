Treatment
20
WLE of primary tumour with minimum 15mm margin
21
Groin node surgery should be undertaken through separate incisions (triple incision technique) to reduce morbidity.
22
The incidence of skin bridge recurrence in early-stage disease is low
23
In unifocal tumours of less than 4 cm maximum diameter where there is no clinical suspicion of lymph node involvement, patients can be safely managed by removal of the identified sentinel lymph nodes.
24
In lateral tumours, only ipsilateral groin node surgery need initially be performed.
25
Contralateral lymphadenectomy may be required if ipsilateral nodes are positive.
26
Superficial groin node dissection alone should not be performed, as it is associated with a higher risk of groin node recurrence.
27
Groin node dissection should be omitted in stage Ia squamous cancer, verrucous tumour, basal cell carcinoma and melanoma.
28
Preservation of the long saphenous vein may reduce both groin wound and subsequent lower limb problems.
29
Surgery is the cornerstone of therapy for the groin nodes in women with vulval cancer.
30
Individual women who cannot be optimised to enable surgery can be treated with primary radiotherapy.
31
Plastic surgery involvement may be required for large defects and when radiotherapy has been used.
32
The vulva is a challenging area for wound healing and faecal and urinary diversion is often required.
33
Sentinel lymph node biopsy should be offered to all eligible women with squamous carcinoma of the vulva.
34
Vulval melanomas need to be jointly managed with the appropriate melanoma MDT.
35
Surgery is the cornerstone of therapy for the groin nodes in women with vulval cancer.
36
Primary and recurrent vulval cancer does respond to chemotherapy but responses are variable and toxicity may be a problem in this population of patients.
37
Future development of targeted therapy with drugs such as erlotinib through mutation testing may lead to improvement in vulval cancer treatment toxicity benefit ratio and provide effective systemic treatment even for the more infirm patients.
