The incidence of preterm delivery in the UK is between 5 and 10%.
4
After one preterm delivery, the risk of preterm delivery in a subsequent pregnancy is 15%
5
High-risk women benefit from screening for vaginal infection. Treatment may be clindamycin or metronidazole for BV.
6
Treatment of asymptomatic bacteriuria reduces incidence of pyelonephritis, does not affect PTB rates.
7
Progesterone therapy and cervical cerclage are proven therapies that reduce PTB in select patients - the situation determines the effect.
8
Guidance on which patient in which situation receives which therapy is highly heterogeneous and will be dictated by local policies and the availability of the therapy.
