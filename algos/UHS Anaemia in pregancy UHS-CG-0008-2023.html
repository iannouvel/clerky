Anaemia during Pregnancy Management Guidelines

"Anaemia is defined as Hb <110 g/L in the first trimester, <105 g/L in the second and third trimesters."




"Fatigue is the most common symptom of anaemia reported by patients."

"Anaemia is diagnosed if Hb <110 g/L in the first trimester or <105 g/L in the second and third trimesters."




"For women and people with haemoglobinopathy, do not start oral iron without checking ferritin levels."



"A previous pregnancy within the last year increases the risk of iron deficiency."

"Women with a vegetarian, vegan, or poor diet are at higher risk for iron deficiency."




"GI conditions like Crohn's disease or ulcerative colitis may affect iron absorption."




"Oral iron preparations are safe and effective but can have significant side effects."
