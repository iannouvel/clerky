If a tubal ectopic pregnancy is suspected, transvaginal ultrasound is the diagnostic tool of choice. If there is a healthy contralateral tube, salpingectomy should be performed. If the b-hCG level is initially less than 1500 iu/l and the woman is clinically stable, expectant management is an option.
