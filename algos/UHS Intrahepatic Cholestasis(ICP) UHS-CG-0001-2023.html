UH Sussex Intrahepatic Cholestasis of Pregnancy (ICP) Guideline

Diagnosis of ICP:

If itching in skin of normal appearance and peak random total bile acid concentration ≥ 19 micromol/L, then consider ICP.

Further Investigations:

If persistent itching with no other apparent cause, then conduct liver function tests and bile acid measurement.

Exclusion of Other Causes:

If ICP suspected, then carry out structured history and examination to exclude other causes of itching and liver dysfunction.

Confirmation of ICP:

If resolution of itching and normalization of liver function tests postnatally, then confirm ICP diagnosis.

Management of ICP:

If diagnosed with ICP, then receive consultant led care, weekly BA & LFTs measurements, and drug treatment as prescribed.

Timing of Birth:

If moderate ICP, consider birth at 38–39 weeks' gestation; if severe ICP, consider birth at 35–36 weeks' gestation.

Postnatal Care:

If itch persists after birth, and LFTs and B As remain abnormal, then investigate for other causes of abnormal liver function.

Subsequent Pregnancies:

If planning subsequent pregnancy, advise about risk of recurrence and perform baseline liver function test and bile acid concentration.
