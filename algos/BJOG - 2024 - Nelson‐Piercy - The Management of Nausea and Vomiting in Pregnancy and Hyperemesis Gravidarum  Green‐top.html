Algorithm for Management of Nausea and Vomiting in Pregnancy and Hyperemesis Gravidarum

  1. Initial Assessment:
    • Assess severity of symptoms
    • Evaluate for potential secondary causes
    • Consider differential diagnosis
  2. Lifestyle and Dietary Modifications:
    • Recommend small, frequent meals
    • Advise to avoid triggers
    • Encourage fluid intake
  3. Pharmacological Management:
    • If lifestyle modifications are not effective, consider antiemetic medication
    • Prescribe a suitable antiemetic based on the patient's condition and preferences
    • Monitor for potential side effects
  4. Severe Cases or Hyperemesis Gravidarum:
    • If symptoms are severe or if the patient has hyperemesis gravidarum, hospitalization may be necessary
    • Provide intravenous fluids and electrolytes
    • Consider additional treatment options such as corticosteroids
  5. Follow Up and Ongoing Management:
    • Monitor patient's condition regularly
    • Adjust treatment plan as needed
    • Provide emotional support and counseling
