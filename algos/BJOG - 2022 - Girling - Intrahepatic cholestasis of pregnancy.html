Clinical Advice:

If Peak Random Total Bile Acid Concentration is 19 micromol/L or more, consider the diagnosis of intrahepatic cholestasis of pregnancy (ICP). Additional investigations are not recommended unless specific criteria are met. Seek hepatologist advice for severe, early, or atypical cases of ICP. Confirm postnatal resolution of ICP at least 4 weeks after birth. If a woman has isolated ICP and a singleton pregnancy, the risk of stillbirth increases above the population rate at 100 micromol/L or more.
