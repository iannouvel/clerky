Care of Women with Obesity in Pregnancy

Algorithm

  1. Initial Assessment

    Assess the woman's body mass index (BMI) at the first antenatal appointment.

  2. If BMI > 30

    If the woman's BMI is more than 30, provide information about the increased risks associated with obesity during pregnancy, labor, and postnatal period.

  3. Nutritional Counselling

    Refer the woman to a dietitian for personalized nutritional counseling and advice on physical activity.

  4. Antenatal Care

    Provide standard antenatal care, with additional monitoring for potential complications related to obesity such as gestational diabetes, hypertension, and preeclampsia.

  5. Labor and Birth

    Discuss with the woman about the potential need for anesthetic review, early epidural, or ultrasound-guided placement of epidural.

  6. Postnatal Care

    Offer additional support for breastfeeding and weight management post-pregnancy. Monitor for postnatal complications such as wound infections, thromboembolism, and depression.
