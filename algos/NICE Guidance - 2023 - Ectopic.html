```html Clinical Algorithm for Ectopic Pregnancy and Miscarriage

Variables

  • Age
  • Pregnancy Test Result
  • Symptoms
  • Ultrasound Findings
  • Serum hCG Levels
  • Previous Miscarriage
  • Medical History

Guidance

  • If serum hCG levels increase >63%, likely developing intrauterine pregnancy; offer ultrasound 7-14 days later.
  • If serum hCG levels decrease >50% after 48 hours, pregnancy unlikely to continue; provide support information.
  • If serum hCG levels decrease <50% or increase <63%, refer for clinical review within 24 hours.
  • Do not use serum progesterone to diagnose pregnancy location.
  • Systemic methotrexate for ectopic pregnancy if no significant pain and specific criteria met.
  • Laparoscopic surgery recommended for ectopic pregnancy; salpingectomy generally preferred.
  • Anti-D immunoglobulin for all rhesus-negative women undergoing procedures.
  • Use ultrasound to diagnose tubal ectopic pregnancy; consider clinical presentation and serum hCG levels.
  • Micronized vaginal progesterone for women with early pregnancy bleeding and previous miscarriage.
  • Medical management of miscarriage with mifepristone and misoprostol recommended.
  • Expectant management for clinically stable women with small ectopic pregnancies.
```
