```html Clinical Algorithm for Ectopic Pregnancy and Miscarriage

Variables

  • Age
  • Pregnancy Test Result
  • Symptoms
  • Ultrasound Findings
  • Serum hCG Levels
  • Previous Miscarriage
  • Clinical Stability

Guidance

For women of reproductive age, consider offering a pregnancy test even with non-specific symptoms. Refer women with positive pregnancy tests and specific symptoms for further assessment.

Use ultrasound scans to diagnose viable intrauterine and tubal ectopic pregnancies. Consider signs indicating tubal ectopic pregnancy during ultrasound scans.

Take two serum hCG measurements 48 hours apart for management of pregnancy of unknown location. Provide written information to women on accessing emergency care.

For a woman with an increase in serum hCG levels greater than 63%, offer a transvaginal ultrasound scan to determine the location of the pregnancy. If a viable intrauterine pregnancy is confirmed, offer routine antenatal care.

For a woman with a decrease in serum hCG levels greater than 50% after 48 hours, provide information about where she can access support and counseling services.

For a woman with a decrease in serum hCG levels less than 50%, or an increase less than 63%, refer her for clinical review in the early pregnancy assessment service within 24 hours.

Do not use serum progesterone measurements as an adjunct to diagnose either viable intrauterine pregnancy or ectopic pregnancy.

Medical management of miscarriage includes the use of mifepristone and misoprostol to reduce the need for surgical intervention.
```
