Clinical Advice

Consider the variables to guide treatment decisions.

If the patient is older, has multiple comorbidities, and prefers non-invasive treatments, then consider conservative management for a less severe disease presentation. Else, if the patient is younger, has no comorbidities, and prefers aggressive treatment, then consider more invasive interventions for a severe disease presentation based on laboratory results and imaging findings.

If the patient has not responded well to previous treatments and has experienced adverse reactions to medications, then consider alternative therapies with caution. Else, if the patient has no contraindications and cost is not a limiting factor, then follow guidelines from regulatory bodies and expert opinion to guide treatment decisions.

If resources are limited and evidence-based medicine supports a certain approach, then prioritize treatments that are most cost-effective and readily available. Else, if there are conflicting opinions or lack of clear guidance, then consult with a multidisciplinary team to develop a personalized treatment plan for the patient.
