Guidance

Chorionicity Assessment: Determine by the number of placental masses and membrane appearance before 14 weeks of gestation.

Ultrasound Exam Frequency: Every 2 weeks from 16+0 weeks onwards until delivery.

Screening for Chromosomal Abnormalities: Offered in monochorionic twins, including Down syndrome in the first trimester.

Management of TTTS: Managed in fetal medicine centers with specialist expertise; consider fetoscopic laser ablation before 26 weeks of gestation.

Delivery Timing and Method: Elective delivery from 36+0 weeks; MCMA twins by caesarean section between 32+0 and 34+0 weeks.

Monitoring for Fetal Anemia: Use Doppler ultrasonography post-single intrauterine death.

Management of sGR: Surveillance every 2 weeks with fetal Doppler assessment; delivery planning based on type of sGR and fetal growth velocity.

Screening for TAPS: After fetoscopic laser ablation for TTTS.

Delivery in Cases of Single Twin Demise: Consider fetal MRI to detect neurological morbidity and manage in fetal medicine centers.
