```html Management of Monochorionic Twin Pregnancy
Variable Guidance
Initial Ultrasound All women with a twin pregnancy should have an ultrasound between 11+0 weeks and 13+6 weeks to assess fetal viability, gestational age, and chorionicity.
Chorionicity Assessment Determine based on the number of placental masses and membrane appearance before 14 weeks.
Fetal Ultrasound Assessments every 2 weeks from 16+0 weeks onwards until delivery.
Screening for Abnormalities Offer screening for chromosomal and structural abnormalities.
TTTS Monitoring Monitor for twin-to-twin transfusion syndrome and manage in specialist fetal medicine centers.
Delivery Timing Discuss and offer elective delivery from 36+0 weeks.
Single Twin Demise Refer to fetal medicine center for assessment and management.
MCMA Twins Delivery Deliver by caesarean section between 32+0 and 34+0 weeks.
Higher Order Multiples Discuss selective reduction in higher order multiple pregnancies with monochorionicity.
Discordant Abnormalities Management Manage in fetal medicine centers.
Sonographer Training Train sonographers to establish chorionicity and recognize complications.
Screening for Down Syndrome Offer screening in monochorionic twins.
Ultrasound Scans Routine scans recommended between 18 and 20+6 weeks of gestation.
sGR Screening Screen for selective growth restriction with EFW discordance and umbilical artery Doppler evaluation.
Quintero Staging for TTTS Use the Quintero system to stage TTTS and manage accordingly in fetal medicine centers.
Fetoscopic Laser Ablation Recommended for TTTS presenting before 26 weeks of gestation.
Delivery Post-TTTS Treatment Consider delivery between 34+0 and 36+6 weeks after successful TTTS treatment.
sGR Management Manage selective growth restriction in regional fetal medicine centers.
```
