- If a woman has a twin pregnancy, she should have an ultrasound examination between 11+0 weeks and 13+6 weeks to assess fetal viability, gestational age, and chorionicity. If chorionicity is determined based on the number of placental masses and membrane appearance before 14 weeks, then fetal ultrasound assessments should occur every 2 weeks from 16+0 weeks onwards until delivery.

- If screening for chromosomal abnormalities and structural abnormalities is offered and specific complications such as twin-to-twin transfusion syndrome are monitored, then treatment for conditions like TTTS should be managed in fetal medicine centers with specialist expertise.

- If delivery of monochorionic twins is discussed, then elective delivery should be offered from 36+0 weeks. If there is a single twin demise, referral to a fetal medicine center is recommended for assessment and management.

- If monitoring for fetal anaemia after intrauterine death is done using Doppler ultrasonography, then MCMA twins should be delivered by caesarean section between 32+0 and 34+0 weeks.

- If there are higher order multiple pregnancies with monochorionicity, then selective reduction may be required. If there are discordant abnormalities in monochorionic pregnancies, they should be promptly assessed and managed in fetal medicine centers.
