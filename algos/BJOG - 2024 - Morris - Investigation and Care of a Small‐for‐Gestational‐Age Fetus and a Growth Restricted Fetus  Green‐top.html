Clinical Guidance on Small-for-Gestational-Age (SGA) and Growth-Restricted Fetuses

Guidance Summary

Guidance includes assessing women for risk factors, reducing smoking, and monitoring fetal growth. Differentiating between SGA and FGR is crucial, with FGR indicating a pathological restriction of genetic growth potential. Surveillance and management strategies vary based on the gestational age of onset of FGR.

Guidance for Fetal Growth Monitoring

If maternal history, symptoms, or Doppler velocimetry indicate abnormal fetal growth:

  • Consider serial ultrasound scans and Doppler assessments
  • Use standard protocols for ultrasound scanning
  • Ensure accurate plotting of measurements on growth charts

Risk Assessment

If a pregnant woman has known risk factors for FGR, such as previous FGR/SGA newborn or medical conditions:

  • Consider aspirin for reducing the risk of hypertensive disorders
  • Evaluate the need for growth scans based on maternal characteristics

Maternal Factors Associated with SGA

If a pregnant woman has maternal characteristics associated with an increased risk of SGA:

  • Consider monitoring fetal growth with Doppler screening
  • Assess for biochemical markers indicating higher risk of SGA

Interventions and Treatments

For women with low PAPP-A or raised AFP:

  • Consider high-dose folic acid or multiple micronutrient supplementation
  • Avoid high protein supplementation

Monitoring and Surveillance

If fetal growth is a concern:

  • Consider serial ultrasound measurements
  • Use umbilical artery Doppler for surveillance
