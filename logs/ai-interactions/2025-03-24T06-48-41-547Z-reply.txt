**Presenting Complaint:**  
Ms. Doe presents with irregular menstrual cycles and pelvic pain over the past six months. She also reports difficulty conceiving despite trying for the past year.  

---

**History of Presenting Complaint:**  
Ms. Doe describes her menstrual cycles as irregular, occurring every 35–60 days, with heavy bleeding lasting 7–10 days. She reports severe cramping and pelvic pain, particularly during menstruation, which she rates as 8/10 in severity. The pain radiates to her lower back and is associated with bloating and fatigue. She has noticed no improvement with over-the-counter painkillers.  

Regarding fertility, Ms. Doe has been trying to conceive for the past year without success. She had one prior pregnancy five years ago, which resulted in a full-term vaginal delivery without complications. She denies any history of sexually transmitted infections or abnormal cervical screening results.

**Ideas, Concerns, and Expectations:**  
Ms. Doe is concerned that her irregular cycles and pelvic pain may be related to her PCOS and is worried about her ability to conceive. She is also anxious about the possibility of endometriosis, as her sister was recently diagnosed with the condition. She hopes to receive a clear diagnosis and guidance on managing her symptoms and improving her fertility.  

---

**Examination Findings:**  
- General appearance: Obese, no signs of acute distress.  
- Abdominal examination: Soft, non-tender, no palpable masses.  
- Pelvic examination:  
  - Speculum examination: Cervix appears normal, no visible lesions or discharge.  
  - Bimanual examination: Uterus anteverted, slightly enlarged, and tender on palpation. No adnexal masses noted, but there is tenderness in the posterior fornix.  

---

**Impression:**  
1. **Suspected endometriosis** based on the history of dysmenorrhea, pelvic pain, and family history.  
2. **Oligomenorrhea and subfertility** likely secondary to PCOS and possible endometriosis.  

---

**Management Plan:**  
1. **Investigations:**  
   - Transvaginal ultrasound to assess for endometriomas, ovarian cysts, or other pelvic pathology.  
   - Hormonal profile (FSH, LH, prolactin, TSH, AMH) to evaluate ovarian reserve and PCOS status.  
   - Hysterosalpingography (HSG) to assess tubal patency.  
   - Laparoscopy for definitive diagnosis of endometriosis if symptoms persist or imaging is inconclusive.  

2. **Symptom Management:**  
   - Start combined oral contraceptive pill (COCP) to regulate cycles and reduce menstrual pain.  
   - Consider a trial of GnRH agonists if endometriosis is confirmed.  
   - Referral to a dietitian for weight management and lifestyle modifications to improve fertility outcomes.  

3. **Fertility Support:**  
   - Referral to a fertility specialist for further evaluation and potential ovulation induction therapy.  
   - Discuss the role of weight loss in improving fertility and reducing pregnancy-related risks.  

4. **Follow-Up:**  
   - Review in 6 weeks to discuss investigation results and adjust management plan as needed.  
   - Provide contact details for local endometriosis support groups and fertility counseling services.

**Patient Education:**  
Ms. Doe was counseled on the potential diagnosis of endometriosis and its impact on fertility. She was reassured that while her weight and PCOS may contribute to her symptoms, there are effective treatment options available. She was encouraged to quit smoking and engage in regular physical activity to improve her overall health and fertility.
