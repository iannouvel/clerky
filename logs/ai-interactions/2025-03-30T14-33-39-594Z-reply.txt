### **Guideline Reference: BJOG - 2018 - Denison - Care of Women with Obesity in Pregnancy**  
**Full Text of Guideline:**  
1. **Pre-pregnancy and antenatal care:** Women with obesity (BMI ≥30) should receive pre-conception counselling and early antenatal care to address risks (gestational diabetes, thromboembolism, fetal macrosomia).  
2. **Thromboprophylaxis:** Prophylactic LMWH should be considered for pregnant women with BMI ≥40, especially with additional risk factors (e.g., prior gestational diabetes).  
3. **Gestational diabetes screening:** Early OGTT (oral glucose tolerance test) is recommended for women with BMI ≥30, particularly if prior gestational diabetes or elevated random glucose.  
4. **Imaging:** Transabdominal ultrasound may be technically difficult; transvaginal ultrasound should be considered if feasible.  
5. **Delivery planning:** Discuss mode of delivery (higher risk of cesarean) and anesthetic challenges.  
6. **Contraception:** Long-acting reversible contraception (LARC) should be strongly advised postpartum to prevent rapid repeat pregnancy.
