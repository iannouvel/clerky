### **Guideline Text (ESHRE Guideline - 2022 - Endometriosis)**  
**Key Recommendations:**  
1. **Diagnosis:**  
   - Transvaginal ultrasound (TVUS) and MRI are first-line imaging modalities for endometriosis.  
   - Laparoscopy with histology remains the gold standard for diagnosis, particularly for deep endometriosis.  
   - Clinical suspicion alone (symptoms + examination findings) may justify empirical treatment in some cases.  

2. **Medical Management:**  
   - Combined hormonal contraceptives (CHCs) are first-line for pain management.  
   - Progestins (oral, injectable, or LNG-IUS) are alternatives if CHCs are contraindicated.  
   - GnRH agonists with add-back therapy (e.g., tibolone or low-dose estrogen-progestin) should be considered for severe symptoms.  

3. **Surgical Considerations:**  
   - Surgery (laparoscopy) should be considered if symptoms persist despite medical therapy or if fertility is a concern.  
   - Endometrioma excision may improve pain but should be balanced against ovarian reserve risks.  

4. **Fertility & Counselling:**  
   - Early referral to a fertility specialist is recommended for patients desiring conception.  
   - Endometriosis-associated infertility may require ART (assisted reproductive technology).  

5. **Multidisciplinary Approach:**  
   - Psychological support and patient education should be integrated into care.
