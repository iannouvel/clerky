50% of couples are sexually active by 6 weeks postnatal.

The postnatal period is a high risk time for unintended pregnancy.

A short interpregnancy interval can increase risks to mum and baby.

Fertility resumes quickly and contraception is needed from 21 days after birth to reduce the risk of unintended pregnancy.

What can I start straight after delivery?

Hormone or copper coil - Can be inserted up to 48hrs after delivery, works for 5-10 years, safe and highly effective

Implant - Highly effective, Long acting, Safe for most women, can be inserted immediately after birth

Condoms - Easy and safe to use, slightly less effective than other methods, can be used if waiting to start a long acting method

Progesterone only pill - Taken daily, safe for most women, >99% effective

Injection - Highly effective, long acting, repeated every 13 weeks, can be given immediately after birth

Implant - Highly effective, long-acting, safe for most women, can be inserted immediately after birth
