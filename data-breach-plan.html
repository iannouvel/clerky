Data Breach Response Plan

GDPR Compliance Procedure

1. Purpose

This Data Breach Response Plan outlines the procedures to be followed in the event of a personal data breach in accordance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) requirements. The objective is to ensure that we can respond swiftly and effectively to any data breach incident, minimize potential harm to affected individuals, and fulfill our legal obligations.

2. Scope

This plan applies to all personal data processed by clerky, including data processed on behalf of users. It covers all instances where personal data may have been:

  • Accessed without authorization
  • Lost or stolen
  • Damaged or corrupted
  • Disclosed to unauthorized recipients
  • Altered without authorization
  • Made unavailable

3. Data Breach Response Team

The Data Breach Response Team consists of the following roles and responsibilities:

  • Data Protection Officer (DPO): Oversees the response process, makes decisions on notification to supervisory authorities and affected individuals.
  • IT Security Specialist: Investigates technical aspects of the breach, implements immediate containment measures.
  • Legal Counsel: Provides guidance on legal obligations and liabilities.
  • Communications Manager: Handles internal and external communications.
  • System Administrator: Assists in technical investigation and remediation.

4. Data Breach Response Procedure

Step 1: Detection and Reporting (0-6 hours)

Any employee, contractor, or user who suspects or identifies a potential data breach must immediately report it to the Data Protection Officer using the following methods:

  • Email: dpo@clerky.com
  • Phone: [Emergency Number]

The report should include:

  • Date and time of discovery
  • Description of the incident
  • Type of data potentially affected
  • Systems or services involved

Step 2: Initial Assessment (6-12 hours)

The DPO will conduct an initial assessment to determine:

  • If a personal data breach has occurred
  • The nature and scope of the breach
  • Categories and approximate number of data subjects affected
  • Categories and approximate volume of personal data records affected
  • Potential consequences and severity of the breach
  • Need for immediate containment actions

Step 3: Containment (12-24 hours)

The IT Security Specialist, in collaboration with system administrators, will implement measures to:

  • Stop the breach from continuing or expanding
  • Isolate affected systems
  • Secure physical areas if relevant
  • Preserve evidence for investigation
  • Change access credentials if necessary

Step 4: Risk Assessment (24-48 hours)

The Data Breach Response Team will conduct a thorough risk assessment to determine:

  • The likelihood and severity of impact on affected individuals
  • Whether the breach is likely to result in a risk to individuals' rights and freedoms
  • Whether the breach requires notification to the supervisory authority
  • Whether affected individuals need to be notified

Step 5: Notification (Within 72 hours of discovery if applicable)

If the breach poses a risk to individuals' rights and freedoms, the DPO will:

  • Notify the appropriate supervisory authority (e.g., ICO in the UK) within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach
  • Prepare notification content according to Article 33 of GDPR
  • Determine if affected individuals need to be notified (required if high risk to rights and freedoms)
  • Prepare communication to affected individuals if necessary, following Article 34 requirements

Step 6: Investigation and Recovery

The team will conduct a thorough investigation to:

  • Determine the root cause of the breach
  • Document all aspects of the incident and response
  • Implement recovery measures to restore affected data and systems
  • Develop and implement remediation plans

Step 7: Post-Breach Review

After resolving the breach, the team will:

  • Review the effectiveness of the response
  • Identify lessons learned
  • Update security measures and this response plan as needed
  • Conduct additional staff training if required
  • Prepare a comprehensive report for management

5. Notification Requirements

5.1 Notification to Supervisory Authority

In accordance with Article 33 of the GDPR, the notification to the supervisory authority shall include:

  • The nature of the personal data breach including, where possible, the categories and approximate number of data subjects and personal data records concerned
  • The name and contact details of the DPO or other contact point
  • The likely consequences of the breach
  • The measures taken or proposed to address the breach, including measures to mitigate possible adverse effects

5.2 Notification to Affected Individuals

In accordance with Article 34 of the GDPR, if the breach is likely to result in a high risk to the rights and freedoms of individuals, we will communicate the breach to the affected individuals without undue delay. The communication will include:

  • A clear and plain language description of the nature of the breach
  • The name and contact details of the DPO or other contact point
  • The likely consequences of the breach
  • The measures taken or proposed to address the breach, including measures to mitigate possible adverse effects
  • Recommendations for individuals to protect themselves from potential effects

6. Documentation

The DPO will maintain a record of all data breaches, regardless of whether they are notifiable to the supervisory authority. The record will include:

  • The facts relating to the breach
  • The effects of the breach
  • Remedial actions taken
  • The rationale for decisions made during the response process

7. Training and Awareness

All staff members will receive regular training on:

  • Identifying potential data breaches
  • The procedure for reporting breaches
  • Their responsibilities in the event of a breach

The Data Breach Response Team will participate in simulated breach exercises at least annually to ensure readiness.

8. Review and Updates

This Data Breach Response Plan will be reviewed:

  • Annually
  • Following any data breach incident
  • When there are significant changes to our data processing activities
  • When there are relevant changes to laws or regulations
