Summary: Antepartum Haemorrhage

Definitions: spotting is staining / streaking / wiping; minor is < 50 ml and not ongoing; major is 50-1000 ml without shock; massive is >1000mls and/or signs of shock

Kleihauer if > 20 weeks

Discharge at initial assessment if spotting only, no longer bleeding, and no concern of praevia if assessment reassuring, all other cases home after admission, senior review and bleeding settled

Tocolysis can be considered by a senior obstetrician, shouldn't be used with significant concerns of maternal-fetal wellbeing

Following single or recurrent episodes of APH from a cervical ectropion, subsequent antenatal care need not be altered.

Serial scans for any cause of APH other than ectropion

If maternal or fetal compromise from APH suspected, consider general anaesthetic

Following APH, advise active 3rd stage to reduce blood loss, particularly syntometrine if no hypertension and APH due to abruption or praevia.

If massive haemorrhage and/or abruption, consider DIC, send clotting and FBC and seek haem support, consider 4 units FFP and 10 units cryo
