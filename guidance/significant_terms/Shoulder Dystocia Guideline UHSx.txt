Here are the most clinically significant terms and keywords extracted from the guideline, critical for understanding the guidance on **shoulder dystocia (SD)**:

### **Core Definitions & Key Concepts**  
- **Shoulder dystocia (SD)**  
- **Vaginal cephalic birth**  
- **Additional obstetric manoeuvres**  
- **Gentle downward traction (unsuccessful)**  

### **Epidemiology & Risks**  
- **Incidence (~0.6% of vaginal births)**  
- **Perinatal mortality & morbidity**  
- **Maternal morbidity**  
  - **Postpartum haemorrhage (PPH)**  
  - **Maternal trauma**  

### **Risk Factors**  
- **Fetal macrosomia**  
- **Diabetes mellitus (maternal)**  
- **Maternal obesity**  
- **Previous shoulder dystocia (recurrence risk)**  

### **Clinical Management**  
#### **Antenatal**  
- **Risk assessment**  
- **Recurrence prevention planning**  

#### **Intrapartum**  
- **Prompt recognition**  
- **Systematic care**  
  - **Hypoxic acidosis prevention**  
  - **Minimizing fetal trauma**  
- **Emergency management**  
  - **Experienced obstetrician involvement**  
  - **Systematic techniques (e.g., PROMPT)**  

#### **Post-birth**  
- **Maternal monitoring** (haemorrhage, perineal tears)  
- **Paediatric management** (if **brachial plexus injury** suspected)  
- **Follow-up care & counselling**  

### **Training & Documentation**  
- **Training for birth attendants**  
- **Record keeping & reporting**  

### **Appendices (Key Supporting Tools)**  
- **PROMPT management** (Appendix 1)  
- **Shoulder Dystocia Proforma** (Appendix 2)  

These terms encapsulate the critical clinical, management, and risk-related aspects of shoulder dystocia as outlined in the guideline.
