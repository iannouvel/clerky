Here are the most clinically significant terms and keywords extracted from the **Shoulder Dystocia PROMPT Card** guideline, critical for understanding the guidance:  

### **Key Clinical Terms & Keywords:**  
1. **Shoulder dystocia** (primary condition)  
2. **Call for help** (senior MW, Registrar, paediatrician)  
3. **Neonatal resuscitation** (preparation required)  
4. **McRoberts maneuver** (first-line intervention)  
   - Lie flat, elevate legs  
5. **Suprapubic pressure** (first-line intervention)  
   - 30 seconds continuous **OR** 30 seconds rocking pressure  
6. **Attempt to deliver baby** (after each maneuver)  
7. **Evaluate for episiotomy** (if needed)  
8. **Posterior shoulder pressure techniques** (3-step approach):  
   - Pressure on **posterior aspect of anterior shoulder**  
   - Pressure on **posterior aspect of anterior shoulder & anterior aspect of posterior shoulder**  
   - Pressure on **posterior aspect of posterior shoulder**  
9. **Adduct shoulders & push baby into oblique** (alternative maneuver)  
10. **Rotate 180ยบ** (in both directions if needed)  
11. **Remove posterior arm** (advanced maneuver)  
12. **Roll onto all fours** (change maternal position)  
13. **If unsuccessful:**  
    - **Repeat all maneuvers**  
    - **Cleidotomy** (fracture clavicle)  
    - **Zavanelli maneuver** (cephalic replacement)  
    - **Symphysiotomy** (last resort)  
14. **Consultant obstetrician** (must be contacted)  
15. **Critical Warnings:**  
    - **Never apply excessive traction or flexion to fetal head**  
    - **Order of maneuvers depends on clinical judgment**  

### **Most Critical Takeaways:**  
- **First-line:** McRoberts + Suprapubic pressure  
- **Escalation steps** if unsuccessful  
- **Avoid harmful traction**  
- **Consultant involvement mandatory** for advanced interventions  

These terms encapsulate the essential actions, warnings, and decision points in managing shoulder dystocia.
