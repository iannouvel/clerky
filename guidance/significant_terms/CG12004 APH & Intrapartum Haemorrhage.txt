Here are the most clinically significant terms and keywords extracted from the guideline, organized by relevance and category:

### **1. Core Conditions & Definitions**  
- **Antepartum haemorrhage (APH)**  
- **Intrapartum haemorrhage**  
- **Placenta praevia**  
- **Placenta accreta (PAS)**  
- **Placental abruption**  
- **Vasa praevia**  
- **Uterine rupture**  

### **2. Key Risk Factors**  
- Previous **Caesarean section (CS)**  
- Multiple pregnancy  
- Smoking  
- Termination of pregnancy  
- **Morbidly adherent placenta** (risk: placenta praevia + prior CS)  

### **3. Diagnostic & Screening Terms**  
- **Transvaginal scan (TVS)**  
- **Ultrasound scan (USS)**  
- **CTG (cardiotocography)**  
- **Speculum examination** (to exclude cervical cancer)  
- **Anomaly ultrasound** (routine screening)  

### **4. Management & Interventions**  
- **Corticosteroid therapy** (for preterm risk)  
- **Resuscitation** (early anaesthetist involvement)  
- **Emergency management** (acute APH/IPH)  
- **Outpatient vs. inpatient management** (placenta praevia)  
- **Timely delivery** (vasa praevia)  

### **5. Complications & Adverse Outcomes**  
- **Maternal:**  
  - Anaemia  
  - Infection  
  - Shock  
  - **DIC (disseminated intravascular coagulation)**  
  - **HELLP syndrome**  
- **Fetal:**  
  - Death  
  - Preterm birth  

### **6. Monitoring & Follow-up**  
- **Rescan at 32 weeks** (asymptomatic low-lying placenta)  
- **Consultant attendance** (mandatory for CS in placenta praevia)  
- **Documented management plans** (placenta praevia/accreta)  
- **MEOWS (Modified Early Obstetric Warning System)**  

### **7. Abbreviations (Clinically Critical)**  
- **APH** (Antepartum haemorrhage)  
- **PPH** (Postpartum haemorrhage)  
- **IR** (Interventional radiology)  
- **BP
