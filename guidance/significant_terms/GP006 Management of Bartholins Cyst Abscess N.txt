Here are the most clinically significant terms and keywords extracted from the guideline, critical for understanding the management of Bartholin’s cyst/abscess:  

### **Key Pathological Terms:**  
- **Bartholin’s cyst**  
- **Bartholin’s abscess**  
- **Sterile mucous accumulation**  
- **Duct blockage**  
- **Acute inflammatory reaction**  
- **Cellulitis**  
- **Vulval abscess**  
- **Recurrent Bartholin’s abscess**  
- **Diabetes mellitus (screening in recurrent cases)**  

### **Clinical Presentation:**  
- **Soft, fluctuant, painless mass (cyst)**  
- **Acute pain, inflammation, swelling (abscess)**  

### **Management Approaches:**  
- **Surgical intervention (abscess)**  
- **Outpatient management (Word catheter)**  
- **Marsupialisation (with biopsy if >40 years)**  
- **Incision and drainage (larger abscesses)**  
- **Excision of gland (consultant-led)**  

### **Procedures & Interventions:**  
- **Word catheter**  
  - Outpatient procedure  
  - No fasting required  
  - 97% success rate at 6 months  
  - Risk of catheter falling out  
- **Marsupialisation**  
  - Requires **general anaesthesia**  
  - **Biopsy** if patient >40  
  - 5-15% recurrence rate  
  - Higher post-op pain  

### **Antibiotic & Adjunctive Therapy:**  
- **IV antibiotics (if cellulitis)**  
- **No routine antibiotics (unless cellulitis/PID/STI suspected)**  
- **Analgesia**  
- **Warm compress (small abscesses)**  

### **Follow-up & Discharge:**  
- **Discharge plan with follow-up**  
- **Nurse-led discharge (post-op)**  
- **Consult senior clinician (recurrent cases)**  

### **Operational Notes:**  
- **CEPOD pathway**  
- **Day Surgery Unit (ext. 7242)**  
- **General anaesthesia (marsupialisation)**  

These terms encapsulate the core clinical, diagnostic, and therapeutic aspects of managing Bartholin’s cyst/abscess as per the guideline.
