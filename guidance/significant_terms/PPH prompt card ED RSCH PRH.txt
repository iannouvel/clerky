Here are the most clinically significant terms and keywords extracted from the PPH (Postpartum Hemorrhage) PROMPT CARD guideline:

### **Emergency Classification & Response:**  
- **PPH classification**:  
  - **Obstetric emergency** (500-1000mls)  
  - **Major Obstetric Haemorrhage** (1000-2000mls)  
  - **Massive Obstetric Haemorrhage** (>2000mls)  
- **2222 call** (emergency response)  
- **Obstetric emergency team**:  
  - Obstetric team  
  - Labour Ward Coordinator  
  - Anaesthetist on-call  
  - Theatre team  

### **Immediate Actions:**  
- **Full observations**  
- **2 x wide bore cannulas**  
- **Blood tests**:  
  - **FBC** (Full Blood Count)  
  - **Clotting**  
  - **U&Es** (Urea & Electrolytes)  
  - **LFTâ€™s** (Liver Function Tests)  
  - **ROTEM** (viscoelastic haemostatic assay)  
  - **Cross Match 4 units**  
- **IV fluids**  
- **Measure blood loss**  

### **Uterotonics (Medications to Control Bleeding):**  
- **Oxytocin** (10iu IM / 5iu IV bolus)  
- **Syntometrine** (1ml IM)  
- **Ergometrine** (500mcg IM/IV, max 2 doses)  
- **Oxytocin infusion** (40iu in 500ml NaCL at 125ml/hr)  
- **Tranexamic Acid** (1g IV)  
- **Carboprost** (250mcg IM, up to 8 doses at 15min intervals)  
- **Misoprostol** (1000mcg PR, consultant discussion)  

### **Additional Interventions:**  
- **Check placenta** (for completeness)  
- **Check perineum** (for trauma)  
- **Urometer** (urine output monitoring)  
- **Transfer to theatre** (if needed)  

### **Documentation & Reporting:**  
- **Complete Proforma**  
- **Datix** (incident reporting if >1500mls blood loss)
