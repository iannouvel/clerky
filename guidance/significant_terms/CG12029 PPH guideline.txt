Here are the most clinically significant terms and keywords extracted from the guideline, organized by category for clarity:

### **Core Concepts**  
- Postpartum haemorrhage (PPH)  
- Primary PPH  
- Secondary PPH  
- Massive obstetric haemorrhage (MOH)  
- Blood loss volume definitions  

### **Prevention Strategies**  
- Risk factor identification  
- Prophylactic uterotonics  
- Tranexamic acid (TXA)  
- Carbetocin (long-acting oxytocin derivative)  

### **Management Principles**  
- Early intervention  
- Multidisciplinary team approach  
- Emergency calls for PPH/MOH  
- Assessment and resuscitation  
- Shock recognition (late sign)  

### **Pharmacological Interventions**  
- Oxytocin  
- Carbetocin (vs. oxytocin comparison)  
- Tranexamic acid (role in active bleeding vs. prevention)  
- Fresh frozen plasma (FFP)  
- Platelet transfusion  
- Factor VIIa (avoidance in acute management)  

### **Mechanical & Surgical Interventions**  
- Examination under anaesthesia (EUA)  
- Intrauterine balloon tamponade  
- Haemostatic brace suturing  
- Aortic compression  
- Stepwise uterine devascularization  
- Internal iliac (hypogastric) artery ligation  
- Selective arterial embolization  
- Hysterectomy  

### **Monitoring & Resuscitation**  
- Serum lactate measurement  
- Base deficit monitoring  
- Hematocrit and hemoglobin (Hb) levels  
- Blood transfusion protocols  
- Intraoperative cell salvage  

### **Causes & Risk Factors (The Four Tâ€™s)**  
1. **Tone**  
   - Uterine atony  
   - Overdistention of the uterus  
   - Intra-amniotic infection  
   - Uterine distortion  

2. **Tissue**  
   - Retained products of conception  
   - Retained placenta (cotyledon/succenturiate lobe)  
   - Placenta accreta/percreta  

3. **Trauma**  
   - Genital tract lacerations (cervix, vagina, perineum)  
   - Uterine rupture  
   - Uterine inversion  

4. **Thrombin**  
   - Coagulation disorders (pre-existing or acquired)
