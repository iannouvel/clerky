Here are the most clinically significant terms and keywords extracted from the guideline, organized by relevance and category:

### **General Terms**  
- **Asymptomatic ovarian cysts**  
- **Pre-menopausal women**  
- **Post-menopausal women**  
- **Ovarian masses**  
- **Benign vs. malignant**  
- **Risk of Malignancy Index (RMI)**  

### **Definitions & Types of Cysts**  
- **Simple ovarian cyst** (thin-walled, anechoic, no flow)  
- **Haemorrhagic ovarian cyst** (reticular echoes, no internal flow)  
- **Complex cysts** (requires referral regardless of menopausal status)  

### **Diagnostic & Imaging Terms**  
- **Transvaginal ultrasound** (preferred imaging)  
- **CA-125** (tumour marker, interpret with caution)  
- **Alpha fetoprotein (AFP)** (for women <40 with complex mass)  
- **Human chorionic gonadotropin (HCG)** (for women <40 with complex mass)  
- **IOTA Group ultrasound rules** (classification of benign vs. malignant masses)  

### **Menopausal Status & Cyst Management**  
#### **Premenopausal Women:**  
- **Cysts ≤3cm** (normal, no follow-up)  
- **Cysts >3cm ≤5cm** (describe, no immediate action)  
- **Cysts >5cm** (rescan in 6 months)  

#### **Postmenopausal Women:**  
- **Cysts ≤1cm** (clinically inconsequential)  
- **Cysts >1cm ≤5cm** (describe, repeat scan in 6 months)  
- **Cysts >5cm** (gynaecology referral)  

### **Management & Referral Criteria**  
- **All complex cysts** → **gynaecology referral**  
- **Moderate/high RMI** → **discuss with gynaecological oncologist**  
- **Persistent/increasing cysts** → **surgical management**  
- **Aspiration not recommended** (high recurrence risk)  
- **Surgical removal** (avoid spillage, consider oophorectomy)
