Here are the most clinically significant terms and keywords extracted from the guideline, organized by relevance:  

### **Core Clinical Terms**  
- **Diabetes in pregnancy** (pre-existing, gestational)  
- **Glycaemic control** (blood glucose levels, targets)  
- **Insulin therapy** (insulin pump, hypoglycemia, glucagon)  
- **Diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA)** (emergency management)  
- **MODY (Maturity-Onset Diabetes of the Young)** (genetic testing)  
- **Metformin** (alternative to insulin)  
- **VRIII (Variable Rate Intravenous Insulin Infusion)** (stopped at delivery)  

### **Monitoring & Targets**  
- **Blood glucose monitoring** (fasting, postprandial)  
- **HbA1c testing** (postnatal, annual screening)  
- **Continuous glucose monitoring (CGM)** (during labor)  
- **Targets**:  
  - **Fasting**: ≤6.0 mmol/L  
  - **Postprandial (2h)**: ≤7.8 mmol/L  

### **Antenatal & Postnatal Care**  
- **Pre-pregnancy planning** (for pre-existing diabetes)  
- **Multidisciplinary team (MDT) involvement** (obstetrician, diabetic team)  
- **20-week antenatal ultrasound**  
- **Individualized management plan**  
- **Postpartum insulin adjustment** (return to pre-pregnancy levels)  
- **Neonatal hypoglycemia monitoring**  

### **Lifestyle & Prevention**  
- **Healthy diet & weight management**  
- **Lifestyle advice** (postnatal, future pregnancies)  
- **Screening for diabetes** (future pregnancies)  

### **Documentation & Protocols**  
- **Accurate documentation** (insulin in "units")  
- **MDT evidence in records**  
- **Timetable of antenatal appointments**  

These terms encapsulate the critical clinical, diagnostic, and management aspects of the guideline.
