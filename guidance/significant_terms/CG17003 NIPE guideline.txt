Here are the most clinically significant terms and keywords extracted from the guideline, organized by relevance and category:  

### **1. Screening Program & Timing**  
- **Newborn Infant Physical Examination (NIPE)**  
- **Congenital abnormalities** (eyes, heart, hips, testes)  
- **72 hours of birth** (initial screening)  
- **6 to 8 weeks of age** (follow-up screening)  
- **NIPE-trained midwife/paediatric doctor**  
- **SCBU (Special Care Baby Unit)**  
- **Postnatal Ward**  

### **2. Key Responsibilities & Procedures**  
- **Verbal consent** (from mother/birthing parent)  
- **Professional judgment**  
- **Record keeping** (S4N, Personal Child Health Record)  
- **Referral pathways**  
- **False positives vs. missed screening**  
- **Parental presence & explanation**  

### **3. Physical Examination Components**  
#### **Hips (Developmental Hip Dysplasia - DDH)**  
- **Ortolani and Barlow maneuvers**  
- **Hip ultrasound** (if positive screening)  
- **Risk factors** (breech, family history, multiple pregnancies)  
- **Leg length symmetry, knee levels, hip abduction**  

#### **Heart (Congenital Heart Disease - CHD)**  
- **Critical Congenital Heart Disease (CCHD)**  
- **Duct-dependent conditions**  
- **Auscultation areas** (pulmonary, aortic, tricuspid, mitral, coarctation)  
- **Murmurs, extra heart sounds**  
- **Tachypnoea, cyanosis, weak femoral pulses**  
- **Pulse oximetry (<95%, >2% arm-leg difference)**  
- **12-lead ECG** (if murmur present)  

#### **Nervous System & Reflexes**  
- **Moro reflex** (abnormal = CNS concern)  
- **Limb tone & movements**  

#### **Spinal Abnormalities**  
- **Spina bifida (myelocoele/meningomyelocoele)**  
- **Sacral sinuses, spinal hemangiomas**  
- **Spinal X-ray & ultrasound**
