Here are the most clinically significant terms and keywords extracted from the guideline, organized by relevance and category:

### **Core Definitions & Concepts**  
- **Sepsis** (infection + systemic manifestations)  
- **Severe sepsis** (organ dysfunction/tissue hypoperfusion)  
- **Septic shock** (persistent hypoperfusion despite fluids)  
- **Pyrexia in pregnancy/labour/postpartum**  
- **Puerperium** (postpartum period)  

### **Risk Factors**  
- Obesity  
- Impaired glucose tolerance  
- Immunocompromised state  
- Invasive GAS (Group A Streptococcus) infection  

### **Clinical Management**  
#### **General Principles**  
- **Maternal observations** (full set required if sepsis suspected)  
- **Broad-spectrum antibiotics** (early administration)  
- **IV fluid resuscitation**  
- **Source control** (identify/treat infection focus)  
- **DVT prophylaxis**  
- **Stress ulcer prophylaxis**  

#### **Specific Conditions**  
- **Mastitis** (potential hospital referral)  
- **UTI** (urinary tract infection management)  
- **Pneumonia** (identification & treatment)  
- **Skin/soft-tissue infections**  
- **Necrotising fasciitis** (clinical features & urgent treatment)  
- **Gastroenteritis**  
- **Pharyngitis**  
- **Regional anaesthesia-related infections** (rare complications)  

#### **Time-Critical Actions**  
- **First 6 hours** (tasks for severe sepsis):  
  - Antibiotics  
  - Fluid management  
  - Blood cultures & lactate testing  
  - Vasopressors if needed  

### **Monitoring & Escalation**  
- **Fetal monitoring** (if maternal sepsis in labour)  
- **Neonatal risk** (umbilical exam, paediatric consult if sepsis)  
- **Community midwife role** (full review, observations, escalate if unwell)  
- **Hospital referral criteria** (moderate/severe infection signs)  

### **Key Pathogens**  
- **Group A Streptococcus (GAS)** (high-risk in postpartum)  
- Other bacterial causes (genital/non-genital sources)  

### **Multidisciplinary Roles**  
- **Midwives/Obstetricians** (follow protocol)  
- **Management Team** (ensure protocol updates)
