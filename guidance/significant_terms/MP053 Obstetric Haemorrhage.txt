Here are the most clinically significant terms and keywords extracted from the guideline, organized by relevance:

### **Core Conditions & Definitions**  
- **Obstetric Hemorrhage**  
- **Antepartum Hemorrhage (APH)** (Minor: <50ml, Major: >50ml)  
- **Postpartum Hemorrhage (PPH)** (Minor: 500-1000ml, Major: >1000ml, Massive: ≥2000ml)  
- **Placental Abruption**  
- **Placenta Praevia** (grading, elective C-section)  
- **Vasa Praevia**  
- **Women declining blood products** (e.g., Jehovah’s Witnesses)  

### **Management & Interventions**  
- **Resuscitation** (massive hemorrhage protocol)  
- **Oxytocin administration** (post-delivery)  
- **Blood product transfusion** (urgent request, alternatives for refusers)  
- **Thromboprophylaxis** (after bleeding control)  
- **FBC (Full Blood Count)** (pre-discharge)  
- **Coagulation monitoring** (hemoglobin, clotting parameters)  
- **Consultant Obstetrician involvement** (high-risk cases, placenta praevia)  
- **Neonatology support** (emergency delivery)  
- **Debriefing** (post-hemorrhage)  

### **Risk Assessment & Documentation**  
- **Individualized care plans** (for blood product refusal, high-risk PPH)  
- **Antenatal counseling** (risks, blood refusal)  
- **Ultrasound placental localization**  
- **Datix reporting** (>1500ml blood loss)  

### **Key Responsibilities**  
- **Midwives/Obstetricians/Anaesthetists** (protocol adherence, professional judgment)  
- **Labor ward coordinator** (team communication)  
- **Management** (protocol accessibility, review compliance)  

### **Critical Actions**  
- **Maternal observations** (vital signs, vaginal loss assessment)  
- **Fetal heart auscultation** (APH cases)  
- **Elective cesarean** (asymptomatic placenta praevia)  
- **Emergency delivery protocols** (Consultant-led, oxytocin, neonatology)
