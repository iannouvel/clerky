Here are the most clinically significant terms and keywords extracted from the guideline, organized by relevance and category:

### **Core Definitions & Diagnostic Criteria**  
- **Hyponatraemia** (serum sodium < **130mmol/L**)  
- **Severe hyponatraemia** (sodium < **125mmol/L** + **symptoms**)  
- **Symptoms**: headache, lethargy, confusion  

### **Risk Factors & Prevention**  
- **Pregnant individuals** (higher risk)  
- **Fluid intake/output monitoring**  
- **Avoid excess water intake**  
- **Bladder care**  

### **Emergency Management (Severe Cases)**  
- **Medical emergency**  
- **Urgent senior obstetric & anaesthetic review**  
- **ITU/Critical Care Outreach team involvement**  
- **Transfer to Level 2 care (ITU if possible)**  

### **Treatment Principles**  
- **Goal**: Improve symptoms (not normalize sodium)  
- **Strict fluid restriction** (**30mls/hr**)  
- **Accurate fluid balance monitoring**  
- **Hourly sodium checks**  

### **Specific Interventions**  
- **1.8% Sodium Chloride** (ITU-supplied)  
  - **150ml bolus** (only under ITU instruction/supervision)  
  - **2nd bolus (150ml)** if no improvement

### **Monitoring & Follow-up**  
- **Continue fluid restriction until asymptomatic**  
- **Repeat sodium levels hourly**
