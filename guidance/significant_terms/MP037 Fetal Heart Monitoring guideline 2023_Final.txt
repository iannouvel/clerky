Here are the most clinically significant terms and keywords extracted from the guideline, organized by relevance and category:

### **General Terms**  
- Fetal Heart Monitoring  
- Antenatal fetal monitoring  
- Intrapartum fetal monitoring  
- Hypoxia  
- Acidaemia  
- CTG (Cardiotocography)  
- cCTG (Computerized CTG)  
- DR analysis (Doppler Resistance analysis)  
- STV (Short-Term Variation)  
- Conservative measures  
- Expedite birth  

### **Monitoring Methods & Indications**  
- **Electronic fetal monitoring (EFM)**  
- **Intermittent auscultation** (for low-risk pregnancies)  
- **Continuous CTG monitoring** (for high-risk pregnancies)  
- **Telemetry** (when necessary)  
- **Fetal scalp stimulation**  
- **Fresh eyes assessment** (periodic re-evaluation)  

### **Risk Factors Requiring Continuous CTG**  
- Sepsis  
- Epidural analgesia  
- Oxytocin use  
- Maternal tachycardia (>120 bpm)  
- Maternal fever (≥37.5°C)  
- Vaginal bleeding  
- Previous cesarean birth  
- Hypertension  
- Pre-existing diabetes  
- Gestational age >42 weeks  
- Fetal heart abnormalities  
- Reduced fetal movements  
- Meconium-stained liquor  
- Non-cephalic presentation  
- IUGR/SGA (Intrauterine Growth Restriction/Small for Gestational Age)  
- Abnormal liquor volume  

### **CTG Interpretation & Classification**  
- **Baseline fetal heart rate**  
- **Variability**  
- **Decelerations** (Early, Variable, Late)  
- **Accelerations** (≥15 bpm for ≥15 sec)  
- **Bradycardia** (single prolonged deceleration ≥3 min)  
- **White/Amber/Red classification**  
  - **Normal** (No amber/red features)  
  - **Suspicious** (1 amber or 2+ amber features)  
  - **Pathological** (1 red feature)  

### **Actions Based on CTG Classification**  
- **Normal CTG**: Hourly reassessment  
- **Suspicious CTG**:  
  - Escalate to obstetrician  
  - Maternal position change  
  - Consider fetal scalp stimulation  
- **Pathological CTG**:  
  - Urgent obstetric review
