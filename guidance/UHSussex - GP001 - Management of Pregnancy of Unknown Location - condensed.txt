Management of Pregnancy of Unknown Location (PUL)

Key Principles:
- Guidelines are to assist in decision making, not to be prescriptive
- Guidelines have been reviewed by clinicians involved in early pregnancy care
- Scope applies to women with a diagnosis of Pregnancy of Unknown location

Responsibilities:
- Nurses, Midwives, Gynaecologists, and Obstetricians to follow guidance and use professional judgement
- Management Team to ensure protocol is reviewed as required and accessible to staff and service users

Introduction:
- PUL is when a woman has a positive pregnancy test but no pregnancy seen on ultrasound
- Objective is to find the location of the pregnancy and ensure resolution without complications

Principles of Management:
- Consider ectopic pregnancy until location is determined
- Perform specific tests and manage based on symptoms
- Use serum hCG measurements for trophoblastic proliferation assessment
- Provide written information to women with PUL for emergency care access

Further Management:
- Individualized based on clinical signs and symptoms
- Laparoscopy may be indicated in symptomatic women
- Do not treat PUL with methotrexate

All PUL Women:
- Advise to seek emergency care if severe pain or feeling unwell
- Explain suspected diagnosis and avoid travel to areas without medical care
