Labour Ward Staffing (RSCH PRH only) MD089

Key Principles
A protocol is a set of measurable, objective standards to determine a course of action. Professional judgement may be used in the application of a protocol.

Scope
This protocol applies to all staff on both maternity units.

Responsibilities
Midwives & Obstetricians:
- To access, read, understand and follow this guidance
- To use their professional judgement in application of this protocol

Management Team:
- To ensure the protocol is reviewed as required in line with Trust and National recommendations
- To ensure the protocol is accessible to parents and all relevant staff

Introduction
This document provides information and expected standards of the duties and requirements of key individuals on the labour wards.

Labour Ward Coordinators
The Labour Ward Coordinators oversee the safe and effective staffing of the two labour wards, providing day to day management of staff and labour ward environment, ensuring the maternity units are running smoothly, escalating concerns / making appropriate referrals to the obstetric team, anaesthetic team, midwifery management team and supervisor of midwives as appropriate.

Consultant Obstetricians
Presence on labour ward is defined as the Consultant being physically present on labour ward. They are required to be available as a resource for staff on labour ward, provide support and guidance for doctors in training, midwives, and students, and attend emergency situations as outlined in the document.

Anaesthetic cover
The duty anaesthetist is responsible for providing an anaesthetic service 24/7 to both Labour Wards for women requiring various procedures and attending any obstetric emergency to provide anaesthetic support. Arrangements are in place to ensure availability of duty anaesthetist on the labour wards 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.
