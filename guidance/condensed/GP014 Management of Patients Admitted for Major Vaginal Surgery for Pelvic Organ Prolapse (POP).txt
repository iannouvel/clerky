Brighton & Sussex University Hospitals NHS Trust  
Urogynaecology Protocol: GP014 Management of Patients Admitted for Major Vaginal Surgery for Pelvic Organ Prolapse (POP)  
Date of Issue: 31st October 2014 Review Date: 31st October 2017  

Key Principles: A protocol is a set of measurable, objective standards to determine a course of action. Professional judgement may be used in the application of a protocol.  

Scope: This guideline applies to all women attending for major vaginal surgery for pelvic organ prolapse (POP).  

Responsibilities: Nursing staff & Gynaecologists should access, read, understand, and follow this guidance. Management should ensure the protocol is reviewed as required and accessible to all relevant staff and service users.  

Objective Standards: Pelvic organ prolapse involves the herniation of the bladder, rectum, or uterus due to a failure of support. Major vaginal surgery for POP includes various procedures like vaginal hysterectomy and vaginal mesh.  

Pre-operative management, post-operative bladder care, and prescription and follow-up guidelines are provided. Monitoring compliance is essential.  

Please refer to the full guideline for detailed information.
