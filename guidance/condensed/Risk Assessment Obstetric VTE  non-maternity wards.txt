UH Sussex Risk Assessment for Obstetric VTE:
- Pregnant women should have a VTE risk assessment at Booking Appointment and at 28 weeks
- Repeat assessments for all admissions or new risks noted
- Daily risk assessment using specific risk factors
- Antenatal and Postnatal Risk Assessment for VTE
- Consider thromboprophylaxis based on total score
- Reassess for thromboprophylaxis with new risks
- Consult with haematologist for patients with bleeding risk
- Calculate LMWH dose based on weight
- High prophylactic dose for specific weight ranges
- Relative contraindications to Enoxaparin should be discussed with Consultant Obstetrician
