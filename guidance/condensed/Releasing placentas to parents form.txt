Releasing Placentas to Parents - Information Sheet for Parents

- Placenta should be sealed in 2 bags and stored in a leak-proof container for transport.
- Placenta should be stored in a fridge without food for 48-72 hours before disposal.
- Follow standard hygiene precautions when handling placenta to reduce infection risk.
- Avoid contact between blood from placenta and breaks in the skin.
- Placenta can be buried at home, following local guidelines.
- Bury placenta at least 1 meter deep to prevent contamination.
- Placenta should be free of plastic cord clamps and not buried in non-biodegradable containers.
- Placenta should not be buried in locations that may contaminate water supply.
- Placenta cannot be disposed of in domestic waste bin, can be returned to hospital for disposal.
