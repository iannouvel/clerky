The clinical guideline on the Management of Ovarian Cysts in Postmenopausal Women highlights the importance of assessing and diagnosing ovarian cysts in this population. It recommends initial evaluation with serum CA125 levels and transvaginal ultrasound scans, along with a thorough medical history and physical examination. The guideline provides guidance on the management of different types of ovarian cysts, including conservative management for low-risk cases and surgical evaluation for suspicious or complex masses. It also emphasizes the importance of triaging women based on risk factors and utilizing appropriate diagnostic tools. The guideline calls for further research in various areas to improve the management of postmenopausal women with ovarian cysts.
