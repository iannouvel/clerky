The clinical guideline on the management of ovarian cysts in postmenopausal women emphasizes the importance of accurate diagnosis and appropriate management. It recommends initial assessment with serum CA125 levels and transvaginal ultrasound, with consideration of risk factors and symptoms suggestive of malignancy. The guideline outlines criteria for conservative management of low-risk cysts and surgical evaluation for suspicious or complex masses. It also provides recommendations for future research and auditable topics to improve patient outcomes. Overall, the guideline aims to guide clinicians in effectively evaluating and managing postmenopausal women with ovarian cysts, highlighting the need for individualized care and further research in this area.
