The NICE antenatal care guideline emphasizes the importance of comprehensive care for pregnant women, involving partners in decision-making, and providing regular check-ups, information, and support. It includes recommendations on addressing common pregnancy issues like nausea, heartburn, vaginal discharge, and pelvic girdle pain. The guideline also covers monitoring fetal movements, discussing birth preferences, and managing potential risks like breech presentation and unexplained bleeding. It highlights the importance of tailored communication, peer support, and involving partners in care. The guideline aims to ensure pregnant women receive high-quality, personalized care throughout their pregnancy to improve outcomes for both mother and baby.
