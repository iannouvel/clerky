This clinical guideline focuses on the management of suspected ovarian masses in premenopausal women, emphasizing the importance of accurate assessment and referral to gynaecological oncological services when necessary. It highlights the challenges in differentiating between benign and malignant masses and recommends conservative management for benign masses, with laparoscopic surgery as the preferred approach. The guideline also addresses the management of simple ovarian cysts and emphasizes the significance of quality gynaecological ultrasonography in guiding treatment decisions. Various diagnostic tools and risk assessment indices are discussed to aid in the differential diagnosis of adnexal masses. The guideline stresses the need for individualized patient care and further research in areas of clinical uncertainty.
