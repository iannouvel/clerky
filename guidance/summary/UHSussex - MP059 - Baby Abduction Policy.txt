Diagnosis and Presentation:
- Suspected or actual baby abduction in the hospital setting
- Staff in maternity, portering, security, and switchboard departments involved
- Immediate actions: informing security, contacting the police, coordinating the response

Treatment and Management:
- Move parents to a private area
- Check identification bands
- Reassure mothers
- Cooperate with the police
- Manage media inquiries through Trust's Communications Manager and senior management

Monitoring Requirements:
- Audit the guidance after each reported abduction incident

Contraindications and Cautions:
- Failure to follow the protocol in case of suspected or actual baby abduction
- Non-adherence to visiting hours and restrictions

Risk Factors:
- Lack of staff adherence to guidance and protocols
- Inadequate training on baby abduction risks and actions
