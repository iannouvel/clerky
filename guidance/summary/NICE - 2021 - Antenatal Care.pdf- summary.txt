The clinical guideline for antenatal care emphasizes the importance of routine check-ups, information, and support for pregnant women. Healthcare professionals should involve women in decision-making and provide necessary information in various languages. Antenatal appointments should involve partners and provide additional support as needed. The guideline addresses inequalities in maternal care and highlights the importance of assessing risk factors, discussing potential issues such as female genital mutilation and cardiac conditions, and sharing information with GPs. Recommendations for fetal movement awareness, breech presentation, and common pregnancy problems like nausea and heartburn are also provided. Overall, the guideline aims to improve outcomes and provide individualized care for pregnant women.
