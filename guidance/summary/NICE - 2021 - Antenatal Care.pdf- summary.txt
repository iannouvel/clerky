The clinical guideline for antenatal care emphasizes the importance of regular check-ups, information, and support for pregnant women. Healthcare professionals should involve women in decision-making and provide necessary information in various languages. Antenatal appointments should involve partners and provide additional support as needed. Risk factors for various conditions should be assessed, and appropriate interventions offered. Recommendations include discussing fetal movement awareness, birth preferences, and preparing for labor. The guideline also addresses common pregnancy issues such as nausea, heartburn, and pelvic girdle pain. Overall, the guideline aims to provide individualized care, address inequalities, and improve outcomes for pregnant women and their babies.
