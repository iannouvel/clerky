**Shoulder Dystocia Prompt Card Summary**  

Shoulder dystocia is an obstetric emergency requiring immediate action. Key steps include:  

1. **Call for help** (senior midwife, registrar, paediatrician) and prepare for neonatal resuscitation. Communicate with parents.  
2. **Initial maneuvers**:  
   - **McRoberts position**: Lie flat, elevate legs.  
   - **Suprapubic pressure**: Apply continuous or rocking pressure for 30 seconds.  
3. **Evaluate episiotomy** if needed.  
4. **Internal maneuvers** (if initial steps fail):  
   - Apply pressure to the posterior aspect of the anterior shoulder.  
   - Attempt adduction and oblique rotation (180Â° in either direction).  
   - Remove the posterior arm if necessary.  
5. **Change maternal position** (e.g., all-fours) and repeat maneuvers if unsuccessful.  
6. **Last-resort options** (consultant involvement required):  
   - Cleidotomy, Zavanelli maneuver, or symphysiotomy.  

**Critical reminders**:  
- Never apply excessive traction or flexion to the fetal head.  
- McRoberts and suprapubic pressure are first-line interventions.  
- Sequence may vary based on clinical judgment.
