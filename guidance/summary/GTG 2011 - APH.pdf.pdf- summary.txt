The clinical guideline for Antepartum Haemorrhage highlights the importance of prompt assessment and management of bleeding during pregnancy. It emphasizes the common causes of APH, such as placenta previa and placental abruption, and the potential risks to both mother and baby. The guideline outlines the necessary steps for triage, resuscitation, and investigation, including the need for maternal stabilization and fetal monitoring. It also provides recommendations for delivery timing and mode, postpartum care, and potential audit topics and areas for future research. The guideline stresses the importance of teamwork, communication, and adherence to protocols in managing APH effectively.
