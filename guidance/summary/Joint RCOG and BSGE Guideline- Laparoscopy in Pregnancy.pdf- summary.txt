This clinical guideline focuses on the laparoscopic management of non-obstetric abdominal conditions in pregnancy, such as acute appendicitis, gall bladder disease, and benign adnexal tumors. It emphasizes the importance of experienced laparoscopists performing these procedures to ensure optimal outcomes for both the mother and fetus. The guideline discusses the benefits of laparoscopic surgery, including faster recovery, shorter hospital stays, and lower wound infection rates, while also addressing potential risks such as haemorrhage and organ trauma. It also highlights the need for careful anaesthetic management and post-operative care, as well as the importance of a multi-disciplinary team approach for pregnant women undergoing laparoscopy.
