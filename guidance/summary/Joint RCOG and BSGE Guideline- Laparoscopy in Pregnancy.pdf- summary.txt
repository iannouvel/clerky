This clinical guideline focuses on the laparoscopic management of non-obstetric abdominal conditions in pregnant women, such as appendicitis, gall bladder disease, and ovarian tumors. It emphasizes the importance of experienced laparoscopists performing these procedures to ensure optimal outcomes for both the mother and fetus. The guideline discusses the benefits of laparoscopic surgery, including faster recovery and lower infection rates, as well as potential risks such as haemorrhage and organ trauma. It also addresses the controversy surrounding laparoscopic appendicectomy and miscarriage, as well as the preference for laparoscopic cholecystectomy over open surgery in pregnancy. Overall, the guideline provides recommendations for the safe and effective management of abdominal conditions in pregnant women using laparoscopic techniques.
