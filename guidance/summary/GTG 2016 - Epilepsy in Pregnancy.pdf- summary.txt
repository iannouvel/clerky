This clinical guideline on epilepsy in pregnancy emphasizes the importance of proper diagnosis, preconception counseling, antepartum, intrapartum, and postpartum management, as well as contraception for women with epilepsy (WWE). It highlights the need for collaboration between healthcare providers to ensure optimal care for both the mother and baby. The guideline addresses the risks of antiepileptic drugs on fetal development and provides recommendations for monitoring and individualized management plans. It also discusses the impact of epilepsy on pregnancy outcomes and the importance of informing WWE about driving entitlements and disability legislation. Future research recommendations include studying the long-term effects of AED exposure on offspring and optimizing monitoring strategies for pregnant WWE.
