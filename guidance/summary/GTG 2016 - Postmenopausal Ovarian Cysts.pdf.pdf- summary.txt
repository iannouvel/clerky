The clinical guideline on the Management of Ovarian Cysts in Postmenopausal Women outlines the importance of assessing ovarian cysts in this population through serum CA125 levels and transvaginal ultrasound scans. It highlights the need for a thorough medical history, physical examination, and consideration of risk factors for ovarian malignancy. The guideline recommends conservative management for low-risk cysts and surgical evaluation for complex or suspicious masses. It also provides recommendations for future research and auditable topics, as well as useful links and support groups for patients. Overall, the guideline aims to guide clinicians in the evaluation and management of postmenopausal women with ovarian cysts.
