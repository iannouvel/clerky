Assisted vaginal birth guidelines emphasize the importance of proper preparation, assessment, and consent for the procedure. Continuous support during labor, different positions, and delayed pushing can impact the need for assisted vaginal birth. Operators must have the necessary knowledge and skills, with complex cases handled by experienced professionals. Instruments should be chosen based on clinical circumstances and operator skill. Aftercare includes prophylactic antibiotics, pain relief, and education on potential complications. Documentation and follow-up care are crucial, with a focus on maternal and neonatal outcomes. Obstetricians have a duty of candour and should contribute to adverse event reporting for effective risk management.
