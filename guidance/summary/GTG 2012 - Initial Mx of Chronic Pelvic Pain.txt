This clinical guideline focuses on the initial management of chronic pelvic pain in women. It emphasizes the importance of a thorough assessment to identify contributing factors and develop a comprehensive management plan. The guideline highlights the need for a partnership between the clinician and the woman to establish functional goals and monitor progress. Diagnostic criteria for conditions like IBS are discussed, along with the importance of considering non-gynecological components of pain. Various therapeutic options, including hormonal treatments and physical therapy, are recommended. The guideline also addresses the role of psychological factors and past trauma in chronic pelvic pain. Referral to specialists and appropriate investigations are suggested for cases where pain is not adequately controlled.
