This clinical guideline aims to provide evidence-based recommendations for the investigation and management of women presenting with chronic pelvic pain. It emphasizes the importance of identifying factors contributing to the pain, including physical, psychological, and social influences. Various possible causes such as musculoskeletal issues, nerve entrapment, and psychological factors should be explored. Adequate time for assessment, multidisciplinary approach, and thorough evaluation of symptoms and history are recommended. Diagnostic tools like TVS and MRI may be used, and therapeutic options such as hormonal treatments, dietary changes, and analgesia should be considered. The guideline also highlights the importance of patient communication, pain diary, and therapeutic trials before invasive procedures.
