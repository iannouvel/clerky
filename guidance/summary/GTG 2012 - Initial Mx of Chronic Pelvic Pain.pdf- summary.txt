This clinical guideline provides evidence-based recommendations for the evaluation and management of chronic pelvic pain in women. It emphasizes the importance of a thorough initial assessment, including history-taking, physical examination, and appropriate investigations. The guideline covers a wide range of potential causes of chronic pelvic pain, including endometriosis, adhesions, IBS, interstitial cystitis, and musculoskeletal issues. It also discusses various therapeutic options, such as hormonal treatment and physical therapy, as well as the role of self-management techniques and support organizations. Referral to relevant healthcare professionals and consideration of psychological factors are also highlighted. Overall, the guideline aims to assist generalists in providing effective care for women with chronic pelvic pain.
