Diagnosis and Presentation:
- Women of childbearing age with a Body Mass Index (BMI) of 30 kg/m2 or greater are at risk of obesity during pregnancy and childbirth.
- Pregnant women with a booking BMI of 30 kg/m2 or greater should be screened for gestational diabetes.
- Pregnant women with a booking BMI of 40 kg/m2 or greater should be referred to an obstetric anaesthetist for antenatal assessment.
- Women with a BMI of 30 kg/m2 or greater have a pre-existing risk factor for venous thromboembolism during pregnancy.
- Obese pregnant women are at increased risk of stillbirth and prolonged pregnancy.

Treatment and Management:
- Women with a BMI of 30 kg/m2 or greater planning pregnancy should take 5 mg folic acid daily.
- Early dietetic advice is recommended in pregnancy.
- Women with multiple moderate risk factors may benefit from 150 mg aspirin daily.
- Prophylactic antibiotics are advised for women with class 1 obesity or greater undergoing cesarean section.
- Suturing of subcutaneous tissue space is recommended for women with more than 2 cm subcutaneous fat during cesarean section.
- Breastfeeding support is crucial for obese women.
- Postpartum weight loss support and referral to weight management services are recommended.
- A waiting period of 12-18 months post bariatric surgery before pregnancy is advised.
- Consultant-led antenatal care and nutritional surveillance are recommended for women with prior bariatric surgery.
- Folic acid supplementation is advised for pregnant women with obesity.
- Vitamin D supplementation may be beneficial for obese women.

Monitoring Requirements:
- Weight and height should be measured at the antenatal booking visit for pregnant women with obesity.
- Serial symphysis fundal height (SFH) measurements are recommended from 24 weeks of gestation.
- Women with a BMI over 35 kg/m2 should undergo serial fetal size assessments via ultrasound.
- Screening for gestational diabetes is essential for pregnant women with a booking BMI of 30 kg/m2 or greater.

Contraindications and Cautions:
- Anti-obesity drugs are contraindicated in pregnancy.
- Pregnant women with a booking BMI of 40 kg/m2 or greater should have a moving and handling risk assessment in the third trimester.
- Obesity is a risk factor for venous thromboembolism during pregnancy.
- BMI over 40 kg/m2 and immobility increase the risk of pressure sores.
- Caution is advised in cases of failed trial of labor with previous cesarean delivery and maternal obesity.
