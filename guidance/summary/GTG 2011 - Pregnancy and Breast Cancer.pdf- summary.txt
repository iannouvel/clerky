The clinical guideline on Pregnancy and Breast Cancer provides guidance for health professionals caring for women of childbearing age with a diagnosis or history of breast cancer. It emphasizes the importance of multidisciplinary treatment for breast cancer in young women, including considerations for pregnancy during or after treatment. The guideline discusses optimal management of breast cancer diagnosed during pregnancy, contraceptive choices post-treatment, and advice for women planning pregnancy after breast cancer. It also addresses fertility preservation options and the impact of breast cancer treatment on fertility. Overall, the guideline highlights the importance of individualized care and collaboration between oncologists, surgeons, and obstetricians in managing breast cancer in young women.
