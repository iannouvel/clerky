The Reduced Fetal Movements Guideline aims to reduce avoidable stillbirths by ensuring pregnant women and people are aware of reporting reduced fetal movements. Developed by obstetric Leads and a Clinical Effectiveness Midwife, the guideline outlines management recommendations for both community and hospital settings. Key risk factors for RFM include small-for-gestational age fetus and maternal conditions like hypertension. Timely assessment is crucial, with guidelines for different gestational ages ranging from full antenatal assessments to expedited birth options. Process and outcome indicators are provided, emphasizing the importance of continuous monitoring and individualized care plans for women experiencing RFM.
