**Summary of Anti-D and Rhesus Negative Maternity Protocol (MP010)**  

This protocol aims to prevent haemolytic disease of the newborn (HDN) in Rh(D)-negative pregnant women through timely anti-D immunoglobulin administration. Key recommendations include:  

- **Booking visit:** Confirm Rh(D) status; no anti-D required if negative.  
- **28 weeks gestation:** Administer **1500 IU anti-D** (no Kleihauer test needed).  
- **Antenatal sensitising events:**  
  - **<20 weeks:** **250 IU anti-D** (no Kleihauer).  
  - **â‰¥20 weeks:** **500 IU anti-D** (Kleihauer required).  
- **Post-delivery:** **500 IU anti-D** for Rh(D)-positive babies, with additional doses if fetal hemorrhage exceeds 4mL (transfusion team advises).  
- **Intraoperative cell salvage (ICS):** **1500 IU anti-D** (Kleihauer required).  

Contraindications include known anti-D antibodies or hypersensitivity. Midwives and obstetricians must ensure proper documentation and follow-up. The protocol balances standardized dosing with clinical judgment to minimize alloimmunization risk.
