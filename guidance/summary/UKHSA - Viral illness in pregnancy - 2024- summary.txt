This clinical guideline provides guidance on investigating, diagnosing, and managing viral illnesses in pregnancy, focusing on rash illnesses like rubella and syphilis. It covers laboratory investigations, management of different rashes, considerations for pregnant women in contact with rash illnesses, and occupational exposure. The guideline emphasizes the importance of tailored management for pregnant women, clear communication, and documentation. It also includes recommendations for antibody screening, inadvertent immunization, and specific management of infections like chickenpox and varicella. The document highlights the risks of natal transmission of certain infections to the fetus and emphasizes the importance of timely assessment and treatment for pregnant women with rash illnesses.
