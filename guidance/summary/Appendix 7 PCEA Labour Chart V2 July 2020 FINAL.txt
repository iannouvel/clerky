This clinical guideline outlines the management of epidural analgesia during labor. Key recommendations include obtaining informed consent from the mother, monitoring for hypotension, headache, and neurological damage, and prescribing specific medications and settings for patient-controlled epidural analgesia (PCEA). The guideline includes detailed documentation requirements for insertion, initial setup, and ongoing assessment of the epidural block and pain relief. It emphasizes regular monitoring of vital signs, pain relief effectiveness, and infusion volumes. The guideline also stresses the importance of observing for complications, encouraging position changes, mobilization, and hydration, and completing pressure area assessments regularly. Overall, the guideline aims to ensure safe and effective pain management during labor using epidural analgesia.
