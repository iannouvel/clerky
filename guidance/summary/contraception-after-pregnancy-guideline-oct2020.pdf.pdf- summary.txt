The FSRH Guideline on Contraception After Pregnancy provides comprehensive information on contraceptive methods after pregnancy, emphasizing the importance of counseling and information giving to women. It recommends the use of long-acting reversible contraception (LARC) for better effectiveness and highlights the need for a person-centered approach to contraceptive counseling. The guideline covers contraception after childbirth, abortion, ectopic pregnancy, miscarriage, and gestational trophoblastic disease, offering specific recommendations for each scenario. Valid consent, documentation, and considerations for vulnerable groups are also addressed. The guideline is regularly updated based on new evidence and consensus among the guideline development group.
