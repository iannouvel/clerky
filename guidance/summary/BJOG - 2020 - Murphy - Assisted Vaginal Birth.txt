The clinical guideline on Assisted Vaginal Birth emphasizes the importance of preparation, assessment, and expertise in performing assisted vaginal births. It recommends continuous support during labor, different positions during the second stage of labor, and delayed pushing in certain situations to reduce the need for assisted birth. The guideline outlines when to recommend or contraindicate assisted vaginal birth, conditions for safe practice, and the importance of obtaining consent. It also addresses aftercare, documentation, and the importance of training and supervision in performing assisted births. The guideline highlights the risks, benefits, and techniques associated with vacuum and forceps deliveries, as well as the importance of informed decision-making and psychological support for women undergoing assisted births.
