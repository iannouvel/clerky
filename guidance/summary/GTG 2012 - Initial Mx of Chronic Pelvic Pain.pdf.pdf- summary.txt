This clinical guideline provides a comprehensive overview of the evaluation and management of chronic pelvic pain in women. It emphasizes the importance of accurate diagnosis and effective management from the first presentation, considering various factors that can contribute to pelvic pain. The guideline recommends a multidisciplinary approach, thorough initial assessment, and appropriate investigations such as abdominal and pelvic examinations, imaging studies, and diagnostic laparoscopy if necessary. Therapeutic options include hormonal treatments, physical therapy, and self-management techniques. Referral to relevant healthcare professionals and consideration of psychological factors are also highlighted. Overall, the guideline aims to improve outcomes for women with chronic pelvic pain through evidence-based recommendations and comprehensive care.
