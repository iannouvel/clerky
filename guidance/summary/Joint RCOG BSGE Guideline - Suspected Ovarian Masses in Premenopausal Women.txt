This guideline provides recommendations for the assessment and management of suspected ovarian masses in premenopausal women. It emphasizes the importance of early diagnosis and referral to a gynaecological oncologist for suspicious cases. Conservative management and laparoscopic techniques are recommended for benign masses, with specific guidelines for follow-up and surgical intervention based on the size and characteristics of the mass. Serum CA-125 levels and ultrasound findings are key factors in assessing the risk of malignancy, with additional risk assessment tools available. The guideline also addresses the role of laparoscopic surgery in managing ovarian masses, highlighting the importance of experienced surgeons and minimizing spillage of cyst contents to prevent potential malignancy.
