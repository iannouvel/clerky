The UH Sussex Perinatal Mental Health Guideline offers comprehensive guidance for healthcare providers on detecting and managing mental health issues in pregnant individuals and women during pregnancy and the postnatal period. The guideline emphasizes the importance of early identification and management of mental health problems to reduce perinatal morbidity and mortality. It provides detailed protocols for healthcare professionals to follow, including referral pathways and the involvement of specialist services. The guideline also addresses the use of psychotropic medications during pregnancy and breastfeeding, highlighting specific considerations for various medications. Additionally, it outlines the roles and responsibilities of healthcare staff in ensuring the emotional well-being of pregnant individuals and their families.
