The UH Sussex Perinatal Mental Health Guideline is a comprehensive document providing guidance for healthcare providers on detecting and managing mental health issues in pregnant individuals and women during the perinatal period. It emphasizes the importance of early identification and intervention to reduce perinatal morbidity and mortality. The guideline outlines pathways for care, including referral to specialist services for those with severe mental health difficulties. It also addresses medication management during pregnancy and breastfeeding, highlighting specific cautions for certain medications. The document includes protocols for healthcare professionals to follow, ensuring the emotional wellbeing of pregnant individuals and promoting maternal and infant health.
