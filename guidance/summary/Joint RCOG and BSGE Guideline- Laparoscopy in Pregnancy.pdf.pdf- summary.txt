This clinical guideline focuses on the laparoscopic management of non-obstetric abdominal conditions in pregnancy, including appendicitis, gall bladder disease, and benign adnexal tumors. It emphasizes the importance of experienced laparoscopists performing these procedures to ensure optimal outcomes for both the mother and fetus. The guideline discusses the benefits of laparoscopic surgery, such as faster recovery and lower infection rates, as well as potential risks including hemorrhage and organ trauma. It also addresses controversies surrounding laparoscopic appendicectomy and cholecystectomy in pregnancy, highlighting the need for further research to determine the best approach. Overall, the guideline provides comprehensive recommendations for the safe and effective use of laparoscopic surgery in pregnant women.
