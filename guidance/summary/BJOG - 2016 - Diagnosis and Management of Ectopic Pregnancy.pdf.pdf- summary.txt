The clinical guideline emphasizes the importance of transvaginal ultrasound for diagnosing and managing various types of ectopic pregnancies, including tubal, cervical, caesarean scar, interstitial, cornual, ovarian, abdominal, and heterotopic pregnancies. It recommends specific criteria for ultrasound diagnosis and management, as well as the use of serum beta-human chorionic gonadotrophin (b-hCG) levels in planning treatment. The guideline discusses surgical, medical, and expectant management options based on the type and stability of the ectopic pregnancy. It also addresses the need for appropriate training for clinicians and the importance of offering support and counseling to women undergoing treatment for ectopic pregnancy.
