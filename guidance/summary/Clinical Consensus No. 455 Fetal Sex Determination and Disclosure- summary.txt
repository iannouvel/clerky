Clinical Consensus No. 455 emphasizes the importance of determining fetal sex during obstetric ultrasound examinations and respecting parental wishes regarding disclosure. Health care providers are encouraged to include fetal sex determination as part of routine second-trimester ultrasounds and to be aware of increasing patient access to ultrasound reports. The guideline aims to improve perinatal outcomes by diagnosing fetal genital anomalies and respecting women's autonomy over personal health information. Key messages include the importance of determining fetal sex, respecting parental wishes, and ensuring ultrasound reports clearly indicate the presence of fetal sex information for parents to choose whether to read further.
