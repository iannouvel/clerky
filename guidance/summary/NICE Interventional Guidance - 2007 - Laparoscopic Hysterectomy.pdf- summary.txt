This clinical guideline emphasizes the safety and effectiveness of laparoscopic hysterectomy techniques, while highlighting the increased risks of urinary tract injury and severe bleeding compared to open surgery. Specialized training is essential for healthcare professionals performing these procedures. Hysterectomy is recommended for various benign conditions and cancers of the uterus and ovaries, with different laparoscopic procedures outlined. Despite the benefits of reduced hospital stay and quicker recovery with laparoscopic techniques, there is a higher incidence of major operative hemorrhage and urinary tract injuries. Healthcare providers should carefully consider this guidance when deciding on the best approach for their patients.
