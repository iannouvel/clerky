The clinical guideline for the management of breech presentation at term emphasizes the importance of offering external cephalic version (ECV) to women with breech presentation unless there is a contraindication. It discusses the risks and benefits of ECV, as well as the implications for mode of delivery. The guideline also covers the risks and benefits of planned vaginal breech delivery versus planned caesarean section, highlighting the small reduction in perinatal mortality with caesarean section. It stresses the need for skilled birth attendants for safe vaginal breech birth and provides recommendations for intrapartum management, including the use of continuous fetal monitoring. The guideline also addresses the importance of individualized assessment and counseling for women considering their delivery options.
