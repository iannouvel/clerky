The antenatal care guideline emphasizes the importance of routine check-ups, information, and support for pregnant women, involving them in decision-making and providing necessary information. It highlights the need for early pregnancy health information, assessing risk factors, and offering various screenings and interventions. The guideline also addresses inequalities in care, such as increased risks for certain ethnic backgrounds and deprived areas. Recommendations include discussing fetal movements, birth preferences, and potential complications, as well as providing support for common pregnancy-related issues like nausea, heartburn, and pelvic girdle pain. Overall, the guideline aims to improve outcomes and provide individualized care for pregnant women.
