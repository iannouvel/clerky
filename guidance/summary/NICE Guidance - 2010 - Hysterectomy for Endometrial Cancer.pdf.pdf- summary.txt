Laparoscopic hysterectomy is a safe and effective option for endometrial cancer, performed by a specialized team with advanced skills. It offers a less invasive approach and shorter recovery time compared to open surgery, with similar survival rates. Patients may experience shorter hospital stays, but potential complications such as bladder dysfunction and abscesses should be considered. Conversion to open surgery and postoperative complications are reported, emphasizing the need for careful patient selection and specialized training for clinicians. Overall, laparoscopic hysterectomy is a viable option for endometrial cancer treatment, offering benefits in recovery and outcomes.
