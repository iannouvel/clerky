This clinical guideline provides comprehensive information on the investigation, diagnosis, and management of viral illnesses (including syphilis) in pregnant women. It covers a range of topics such as rash illnesses, occupational exposure, antibody screening, and inadvertent immunization during pregnancy. The guidance emphasizes the importance of prompt laboratory investigations, referral for medical management, and considerations for pregnant women in contact with rash illnesses. It also highlights the risks associated with specific viral infections during pregnancy, such as congenital rubella syndrome and fetal varicella syndrome. Healthcare professionals are advised to make management decisions in a specialist fetal medicine unit for infections causing congenital pathology. The document also includes recommendations for screening, diagnostic tests, and vaccination status assessment for pregnant women.
