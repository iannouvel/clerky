This clinical guideline provides comprehensive guidance on investigating, diagnosing, and managing viral illnesses in pregnancy, focusing on rash illnesses such as rubella, parvovirus B19, and measles. It emphasizes the importance of laboratory testing, specialist advice, and monitoring for pregnant women with confirmed infections. The document also covers the risks associated with maternal infections, such as natal transmission and congenital syndromes, and provides recommendations for managing exposures and occupational risks. Additionally, it highlights the importance of vaccination history, screening, and appropriate management decisions, including referrals to specialist units for early pregnancy infections. The guideline does not cover HIV or herpes simplex virus infections in pregnancy.
