Diagnosis of spontaneous rupture of membranes is made by maternal history and sterile speculum examination; consider IGFBP-1 or PAMG-1 test if no amniotic fluid observed. Antibiotic (preferably erythromycin) given for 10 days or until established labor in PPROM; offer corticosteroids to women between 24+0 and 33+6 weeks gestation. Use clinical assessment, maternal blood tests, and fetal heart rate to diagnose chorioamnionitis; offer expectant management until 37+0 weeks in women with no contraindications. Offer intravenous magnesium sulfate to women with PPROM in established labor between 24+0 and 29+6 weeks gestation. Neonatologists should be informed when PPROM diagnosis confirmed, and delivery anticipated; women should meet with neonatologist antenatally. Erythromycin for 10 days following PPROM diagnosis; antenatal corticosteroids offered from 24+0 to 35+6 weeks gestation. Corticosteroids considered between 24+0 and 25+6 weeks; magnesium sulfate offered between 24+0 and 29+6 weeks gestation. Tocolysis not recommended; outpatient care considered individually. Amnioinfusion not recommended; emotional support offered; further research needed on PPROM management.
