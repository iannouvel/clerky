Summary:
Early diagnosis and treatment of malaria in pregnant women is crucial to prevent adverse outcomes for both the woman and fetus. Symptoms include fever, chills, and nausea, and diagnosis is done through blood films or rapid tests. Severe malaria in pregnancy should be treated promptly with IV artesunate or quinine, while uncomplicated cases can be treated with quinine and clindamycin. Expert advice should be sought for severe cases, and treatment should not be delayed. Different treatment regimens are recommended for different types of malaria, with a focus on safety and efficacy in pregnancy. Monitoring and follow-up are essential to ensure successful treatment.
